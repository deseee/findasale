# Estate Sale Organizer Guide

Welcome to FindA.Sale! This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to create and manage a successful estate sale on our platform.

## Getting Started

### Creating Your Account

Head to FindA.Sale and sign up for an account. You'll be asked to provide your name, email address, and a password. Once you've confirmed your email, you're ready to get started.

### Setting Up Your Organizer Role

When you first log in, you'll be prompted to set up your organizer profile. This is where you'll add your business name, a profile photo, and a bio that shoppers will see when browsing your sales. A professional photo and welcoming bio help build trust with your customers.

## Creating a Sale

### Sale Basics

To create a new sale, click the "Create Sale" button in your dashboard. You'll need to fill in the following information:

**Sale Title.** Choose a clear, descriptive title. Examples: "Oak Valley Estate Collection" or "Downtown Victoriana — May 5-7." Shoppers search for sales by location and keywords, so be specific.

**Description.** Write a brief overview of what shoppers can expect. Mention any standout items (antique furniture, vintage jewelry, collectibles), the condition of the goods, and any special details about the property or sale experience.

**Sale Dates.** Specify the start and end dates. This tells shoppers when the sale is open and helps our system organize your inventory timeline.

**Address and Location.** Provide the street address where the sale will be held. You can also add location notes like "Behind the blue gate" or "Lower level entrance."

**Sale Type.** Choose between a regular sale or auction. Regular sales let shoppers purchase items immediately at your listed prices. Auctions run for a set period and require bidding. You'll set the auction end time when you list auction items.

### Making Your Sale Discoverable

Once your sale is created, it appears on the FindA.Sale map and in local search results. You can share the sale link directly with friends and family, or use the QR code marketing feature (see below) to drive foot traffic.

## Adding Items

### Uploading Photos

The first step in adding an item is uploading clear, well-lit photos. You can upload multiple photos per item—use your first photo as the main thumbnail. Here's the process:

1. Click "Add Item" in your sale.
2. Click the upload area to select photos from your device, or drag and drop them.
3. Arrange the photos in the order you want them displayed. Drag photos to reorder them.
4. Once uploaded, proceed to the next step.

Tip: Use natural light when photographing items. Close-up shots of details (maker's marks, condition issues, signatures) help shoppers make confident purchasing decisions.

### AI Auto-Tagging

After you upload photos, our AI will automatically suggest tags and a category for the item. For example, a photo of a vintage lamp might be tagged as "Lighting," "Vintage," and "Brass." Review these suggestions and adjust as needed. Accurate tags make your items easy to find when shoppers search.

### Editing Title, Description, and Tags

Give each item a clear, accurate title. Instead of "Old Dish," try "Blue Willow China Serving Plate." Write a brief description noting any notable features, condition issues, or maker information. Add custom tags if the AI missed something—collectors often search for specific styles like "Mid-Century Modern" or "Art Deco."

### Setting Price

Price items competitively. Research comparable items online if you're unsure of fair market value. Shoppers expect estate sale pricing to be reasonable but not rock-bottom. You can always adjust prices before the sale opens.

### Assigning Category

Select a category from our standard list (Furniture, Glassware, Jewelry, Books, Art, etc.). Categories help organize your inventory and make shopping easier for visitors.

### Marking as Auction Item

If you're running an auction sale, mark items you want to auction. For auction items, you'll set a starting bid price instead of a fixed price. Auction items appear in a dedicated section of your sale and are subject to a 7% platform fee instead of the regular 5% fee (see Payouts below).

## Managing Inventory

### Editing Items

You can update item details any time before the sale starts—and even during the sale if needed. Click an item to open its detail panel, then edit the title, description, price, tags, or photos.

### Reordering Photos

Within an item's photo gallery, you can drag photos to change their order. The first photo becomes the thumbnail that shoppers see in browse view.

### Marking Items Sold

When an item sells or is no longer available, mark it as sold. This removes it from shoppers' search results and prevents duplicate purchases. You can mark items sold manually or through the purchase notifications system.

## Auction Items

### How Bidding Works

When you create an auction sale, shoppers place bids on items rather than buying at a fixed price. Each bid must exceed the previous bid by your minimum increment (typically $1–$5, depending on item value). Bidding continues until the auction end time, at which point the highest bidder wins.

### Platform Fees

Auction items carry a 7% platform fee on the final sale price, compared to 5% for regular items. This fee is deducted from your payout.

### Settlement

After the auction ends, we notify winners and process payments through your Stripe Connect account. Payouts happen within 2–3 business days, minus the platform fee.

## Shopper Management

### Holds and Reservations

Shoppers can request a hold or reservation on items before the sale opens. If you allow holds, the item is marked as "held" and reserved for that shopper. You can accept or decline hold requests from your dashboard. Holds help build excitement and give serious buyers confidence that their items are waiting.

### Responding to Messages

Shoppers can message you directly with questions about items or logistics. Check your inbox regularly and respond promptly. Clear communication builds trust and reduces cancellations.

## Payouts

### Stripe Connect Setup

Before you can receive payments, connect your bank account through Stripe Connect. This is a one-time setup that takes about 5 minutes. Log in to your FindA.Sale account, go to Settings → Payouts, and click "Connect Stripe." You'll be guided through Stripe's verification process.

### Fee Structure

FindA.Sale charges a platform fee on every sale. Regular items incur a 5% fee; auction items incur a 7% fee. For example, a $100 item with a 5% fee nets you $95.

### Instant Payouts

Payments are processed through your connected Stripe account within 2–3 business days of purchase. With Stripe's instant payout feature, you can withdraw funds immediately if needed (note: Stripe charges a small fee for instant payouts).

## Push Notifications

### Enabling and Disabling

Shoppers can opt into notifications for your sales. You control whether your sale sends notifications by toggling the notification setting in your sale's settings. Notifications are sent when the sale starts, when popular items near their end time, and when new shoppers follow your account.

To manage your personal notification preferences, visit Settings → Notifications and toggle on or off for each type of alert.

## QR Code Marketing

### Generating Your Sale QR Code

Every sale has a unique QR code that links directly to its details page. You can print this QR code and display it at the physical location, in local ads, or on social media. When shoppers scan the code with their phone camera, they're taken directly to your sale and can browse your inventory, set holds, and enable notifications.

### Using the QR Code

Print QR codes on posters, flyers, or lawn signs to drive foot traffic. You can also share the QR code image on Facebook or Instagram. The larger the code, the easier it is to scan.

## CSV Export

### Exporting Your Inventory

You can export your entire sale inventory as a CSV file for record-keeping, accounting, or data analysis. Go to your sale dashboard, click "Export," and download the file. It will contain all item titles, descriptions, prices, categories, tags, and sold status.

## Referral Program

### Sharing Your Referral Link

Every organizer account comes with a unique referral link. Share this link with friends, family, or other estate sale operators. When someone signs up using your link and creates their first paid sale, you earn a $5 credit toward your next platform fees.

### Earning Credits

There's no limit to how many referrals you can share. Credits accumulate in your account and are automatically applied to your next invoice.

## Tips for a Successful Sale

Here are a few best practices to maximize your success on FindA.Sale:

**Be Detailed.** Shoppers appreciate accurate descriptions and multiple clear photos. If an item has condition issues, mention them upfront—transparency builds trust.

**Price Fairly.** Research comparable items online. Competitive pricing attracts more buyers and moves inventory faster.

**Respond Quickly.** Answer questions and messages promptly. Quick, friendly communication encourages purchases and builds your reputation.

**Use Tags Wisely.** Add tags that shoppers actually search for. "Vintage," "Collectible," and "Handmade" are popular; niche tags like "Art Deco" or "Tole Ware" help collectors find exactly what they want.

**Photograph Everything.** Good lighting and clear angles matter. If an item has maker marks or signatures, photograph those details too.

**Launch on Time.** Let shoppers know your exact start date and time. Consistency builds trust and loyalty.

**Follow Up.** After the sale, consider sending a thank-you message to repeat buyers or followers. Building relationships leads to repeat business.

Good luck with your estate sales!
