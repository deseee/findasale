# The Collector's Guild — Brand Voice & Copy Language Framework

**Status:** Board Ready | **Session 259** | **Last Updated:** 2026-03-23

---

## Overview

The Collector's Guild gamification system is FindA.Sale's premium shopper experience. It transforms transactional browsing into a narrative-driven, achievement-rich community. This document establishes the brand voice, copy patterns, and multi-sale-type inclusive language that will underpin all Guild-related UI, notifications, and communications.

**Core Principle:** The Guild speaks to **collectors first, shoppers second**. Language positions every hunt as discovery, every find as a small victory, and every rank as earned expertise—not arbitrary points.

---

## Part 1: Gamification Brand Voice

### Voice Adjectives & Definitions

#### 1. **Curious**
What it means: Invites exploration without judgment. Emphasizes discovery over destination. Makes each sale feel like an adventure worth taking, not a shopping transaction.

In practice:
- "What treasures are waiting?" rather than "Browse items"
- "Wondering what you'll find?" rather than "Start shopping"
- Creates openness to unexpected discoveries (vintage, niche, one-of-a-kind items)

#### 2. **Graceful**
What it means: Sophisticated but approachable. Respects that collectors care deeply about their finds without gatekeeping expertise. Tone is warm, not snobbish.

In practice:
- "Distinguish rare pieces" rather than "Identify valuable items"
- "Develop your eye" rather than "Learn to spot deals"
- Honors both a $5 Goodwill treasure and a $5,000 estate auction find equally

#### 3. **Steadfast**
What it means: Reliable. Gamification elements feel like earned achievements, not random luck or manipulation. Progress is transparent, fair, and within the user's control.

In practice:
- "Your streak continues" rather than "Keep the momentum going!"
- Progress bars show exact thresholds, not hidden algorithms
- Ranks advance through clear, documented milestones

#### 4. **Communal**
What it means: Emphasizes belonging to a tribe of fellow collectors, not individual competition. Celebrates shared passion, trading knowledge, and mutual discovery.

In practice:
- "Join other Rangers in your region" rather than "Compete on the leaderboard"
- "Share a find with your fellow collectors" rather than "Post to feed"
- Highlight stories of collectors helping collectors

#### 5. **Timeless**
What it means: Speaks to the permanence and lasting value of secondhand treasures, not fast fashion or trends. Reflects the collector mindset: "I'm building a collection, not a cart."

In practice:
- "A find for the ages" rather than "Limited-time item"
- "Your collection grows" rather than "Your purchases accumulate"
- Reminds shoppers they're investing in future heirlooms

---

### Voice Principle: "Sounds Like / Doesn't Sound Like"

#### Example 1: Achievement Unlocked
✅ **Sounds like:** "You've earned the Vintage Verifier badge—you've now spotted fine details across 10 different eras."

❌ **Doesn't sound like:** "Congratulations! You've unlocked Badge #47. +50 XP!" (generic, gamey)

#### Example 2: Encouragement
✅ **Sounds like:** "Three more hunts until you reach Ranger. What's nearby this weekend?"

❌ **Doesn't sound like:** "You need 3 more hunts to rank up. Don't miss out!" (pressuring, FOMO-based)

#### Example 3: Community Gesture
✅ **Sounds like:** "Three other Sages found treasures at the Riverside Sale last week. What did you miss?"

❌ **Doesn't sound like:** "3 players are ahead of you. Grind harder to catch up." (competitive, exclusionary)

---

### Brand Voice Statement for Gamification Layer

**The Collector's Guild is the voice of a community that treasures discovery, respects the hunt, and builds lasting collections across all types of secondary sales. It celebrates earned expertise—whether you're visiting your second hunt or your hundredth—and treats every collector as a member of a tradition that values patience, curiosity, and community knowledge. The Guild acknowledges that estate sales, yard sales, auctions, flea markets, and consignment each have their own magic, and that a true collector thrives across all of them.**

## Part 2: Multi-Sale-Type Inclusive Language Guide

The platform serves five distinct sale types. Estate-sale-first language alienates 80% of the user base. Use this reference table for all copy: notifications, UI labels, help text, emails, onboarding, and agent prompts.

| Concept | ❌ Estate-Sale-Biased | ✅ Multi-Sale-Type Inclusive | Context |
|---------|----------------------|------------------------------|---------|
| **What you attend** | estate sales | sales, hunts, markets | Covers all 5 types equally. "Hunt" is the gamified term. |
| **The event itself** | an estate | a sale, a market, an expedition | Avoid "estate" for yard sales, auctions, flea markets, consignment. |
| **The items you find** | estate pieces, antiques | finds, treasures, pieces | Neutral. Works for $2 yard sale mugs and $2k estate auction furniture. |
| **The sellers/organizers** | estate sale companies | organizers, hosts, curators | Emphasizes the role, not the sale type. All are running a marketplace. |
| **Browsing items** | browsing an estate sale | hunting, exploring, discovering | "Hunt" is the branded term. "Browse" is functional. "Discover" is inspirational. |
| **Buying an item** | purchased from the estate | found, claimed, added to collection | "Found" implies discovery. "Claimed" implies ownership pride. "Added to collection" emphasizes lasting value. |
| **Searching for items** | looking for estate furniture | hunting for [category], searching your area | Emphasize intent (what they want) + location, not sale type. |
| **The community of collectors** | estate sale shoppers, antiquers | collectors, hunters, finders, treasure seekers | Emphasize the identity, not the transaction. |
| **Expertise/knowledge** | estate appraisal skills | collector's eye, hunt expertise, market knowledge | Recognize different types of expertise. "Eye for detail," "knowledge of eras," "market sense." |
| **A great find** | a good estate sale piece | a treasure, a gem, a find | Neutral. Applies equally to thrift store vinyl and heirloom jewelry. |
| **Repeat visits** | estate sale circuit, estate shopping | the hunt, the scene, collecting circuit | "The hunt" builds brand identity. "The scene" localizes community. |
| **New to this** | estate sale newcomer | new hunter, fresh collector | Frame as beginning a hobby, not just shopping. |
| **Regular participant** | estate shopper, picker | seasoned hunter, experienced collector | "Hunter" ties to gamification. "Experienced" respects their journey. |
| **Price negotiation** | haggling, negotiating the estate price | negotiating, discussing price | Some sales allow it, some don't. Use neutral language. |
| **Time spent hunting** | a weekend estate circuit | a hunt, a day of collecting | Emphasize the experience, not the transaction. |
| **Selling items later** | flipping (estate finds) | reselling, trading, curating your collection | "Flipping" is transactional. "Curating" is collector-minded. |

## Part 3: Shopper Ranks — Copy & Flavor Text

Shopper ranks progress: **Initiate → Scout → Ranger → Sage → Grandmaster**

Each rank progression represents deepening expertise, community trust, and collection ambition. Copy must reflect earned advancement, not just point accumulation.

---

### Rank 1: INITIATE

**Rank Description** (appears on achievement unlock screen):
"You've begun your collecting journey. Initiation into the Guild marks the start of deeper hunts, richer discoveries, and a growing eye for what makes a find truly special."

**Unlock Notification** (push/email when shopper reaches rank):
"Welcome to the Collector's Guild, Initiate. Your first steps into a community of treasure seekers begin now. Your collection awaits."

**Tagline** (appears under username in community):
"Initiate of the Guild"

**Unlock Trigger:** Account creation + first hunt completed (visit any sale with a purchase or saved item)

---

### Rank 2: SCOUT

**Rank Description:**
"Scouts excel at finding unexplored territory and spotting items others overlook. You've proven you know where to look and what catches the eye."

**Unlock Notification:**
"You've earned Scout rank. Your pattern recognition is sharp—you're discovering treasures faster than most. Welcome to a growing circle of serious collectors."

**Tagline:**
"Scout of the Guild"

**Unlock Trigger:** 10 hunts completed + 25 finds (purchases or saved items marked as "collection-worthy")

---

### Rank 3: RANGER

**Rank Description:**
"Rangers navigate the full landscape of the hunt. You've explored multiple sale types, regions, and eras. Your knowledge runs deep and your instincts are sharp."

**Unlock Notification:**
"Ranger rank is yours. You've proven yourself across the full spectrum of sales. Your expertise is recognized. Welcome among the guides of the Guild."

**Tagline:**
"Ranger of the Guild"

**Unlock Trigger:** 30 hunts + 75 finds + visits to at least 3 different sale types (estate sale, yard sale, auction, flea market, consignment) + 500 total Guild XP

---

### Rank 4: SAGE

**Rank Description:**
"Sages possess deep knowledge of eras, patterns, and markets. They guide newer collectors and command respect for their refined judgment. Your collection tells a story."

**Unlock Notification:**
"You've ascended to Sage. Your eye for value, rarity, and authenticity is unmatched. Other collectors seek your knowledge. You are a keeper of the tradition."

**Tagline:**
"Sage of the Guild"

**Unlock Trigger:** 75 hunts + 200 finds + 1,500 total Guild XP + active season participation + community engagement (followers gained, finds liked/shared, or advice given)

---

### Rank 5: GRANDMASTER

**Rank Description:**
"Grandmasters have mastered the art of collecting across all sale types and eras. You are a legend in the Guild—a trusted voice, a seasoned eye, a living catalog of the hunt."

**Unlock Notification:**
"Grandmaster—the highest rank of the Guild. You are legendary. Your collection, your knowledge, and your influence define what it means to hunt with purpose and grace."

**Tagline:**
"Grandmaster of the Guild"

**Unlock Trigger:** 150 hunts + 500 finds + 3,500 total Guild XP + sustained Sage rank for 2+ seasons + high community reputation (verified as a knowledgeable collector by platform and peers)

## Part 4: Seasonal Expeditions — Names, Themes & Challenges

Seasonal expeditions reset Guild XP progress and introduce themed hunts. They celebrate discovery across seasons and sale types. Four expeditions per year.

---

### Expedition 1: SPRING — "The Awakening"

**Official Name:** The Spring Awakening Expedition

**Tagline:** "Dust off winter's treasures and seek what the season brings to light."

**Why This Name:** Spring sales are abundant (estate cleanouts, yard sales after winter). Collectors "wake up" to the hunting season. Spiritual overtone suggests renewal and discovery.

**Challenge Examples:**
- **Newborn Treasures** (Find 3 items from the 1950s–1960s — the golden age of mid-century modern)
- **Garden Gathering** (Discover 5 items that would flourish in an outdoor space—garden tools, outdoor décor, plant stands)
- **Color Awakening** (Find items in 5 different primary colors—build a rainbow collection)
- **Floral Find** (Collect 3 items with floral patterns or botanical themes)
- **Spring Surprise** (Visit a new sale type you've never hunted before)

---

### Expedition 2: SUMMER — "The Expedition"

**Official Name:** The Summer Expedition

**Tagline:** "Travel the hunt. Explore new territories. Summer is peak season."

**Why This Name:** Summer is peak estate sale and yard sale season. "Expedition" ties directly to the gamified concept of "hunts." Positions summer as the prime opportunity for deep exploration.

**Challenge Examples:**
- **Region Rover** (Visit sales in 3 different neighborhoods or towns you've never explored)
- **Outdoor Oasis** (Find 5 patio, garden, or outdoor entertaining items)
- **Vintage Vibes** (Collect 4 items from a single era—1970s, 1980s, 1960s, etc.)
- **Collector's Haul** (Visit 5 sales in a single weekend; claim 10+ finds)
- **Rare Discovery** (Find 1 item you've searched for but never encountered before)

---

### Expedition 3: FALL — "The Harvest"

**Official Name:** The Autumn Harvest Expedition

**Tagline:** "Gather what autumn brings. Rich finds fill the season."

**Why This Name:** Fall sales often feature estate liquidations before winter. "Harvest" suggests abundance and reaping what the season offers. Ties to collection-building metaphor (gathering).

**Challenge Examples:**
- **Harvest Home** (Find 5 items for the home—furniture, decor, kitchenware)
- **Warm & Amber** (Collect items in warm tones—gold, copper, rust, burnt orange)
- **Entertaining in Style** (Find 3 items for hosting gatherings—serveware, linens, entertaining pieces)
- **Leather & Wood** (Discover 4 items made from natural materials)
- **Family Finds** (Collect items across 5 different functional categories—home, fashion, books, décor, collectibles)

---

### Expedition 4: WINTER — "The Gathering"

**Official Name:** The Winter Gathering Expedition

**Tagline:** "A season of quiet hunts and thoughtful collecting. Curate with intention."

**Why This Name:** Winter sales wind down; collectors shift to thoughtful, intentional gathering rather than volume. "Gathering" emphasizes community and reflection. Quieter season, deeper focus.

**Challenge Examples:**
- **Heirloom Hunt** (Find 2 items you'd pass to the next generation—pieces with real meaning)
- **Cozy Collecting** (Gather 5 items for comfort—blankets, pillows, candles, mugs, warm décor)
- **Gift Finds** (Discover 3 items that would make perfect gifts—no purchase necessary, just finds)
- **Monochrome Mastery** (Collect items in a single color palette—grayscale, jewel tones, neutrals, etc.)
- **Story Seeker** (Learn the history of 2 finds—ask organizers or research the makers)

## Part 5: Micro-Event Naming & Copy

Micro-events are limited-time hunts tied to holidays, cultural moments, and seasonal celebrations. They provide short sprints (3–7 days) for engagement and novelty. Copy must feel timely, celebratory, and inclusive.

---

### Micro-Event 1: TAX SEASON TREASURE HUNT

**Event Name:** Tax Season Treasure Hunt

**Copy Line:** "Find your deductions—discover vintage ledgers, office gems, and desk treasures. Every find could spark joy... and inspiration."

**Badge Name:** "Audit Enthusiast" (lighthearted; celebrates finding organizational items)

**Duration:** March 15–31 (tied to tax deadline) / April 1–15 (alternative)

**Why This Works:** Plays on the calendar moment with humor. Works across all sale types (everyone has "office finds"). Speaks to mid-century desks, vintage filing, office décor.

---

### Micro-Event 2: SPRING CLEANING FRENZY

**Event Name:** Spring Cleaning Surge

**Copy Line:** "One person's spring cleaning is another's treasure chest. Find the items that make a space shine—and save money doing it."

**Badge Name:** "Refresh Master"

**Duration:** April 1–30

**Why This Works:** Ties to real user behavior (spring cleanups). Emphasizes value (buying pre-owned vs. new). Works for furniture, décor, kitchenware, textiles—across all sale types.

---

### Micro-Event 3: SUMMER ROAD TRIP EXPEDITION

**Event Name:** Summer Road Trip Expedition

**Copy Line:** "Pack light, find everywhere. Discover treasures 50+ miles from home. Turn your travels into a collecting adventure."

**Badge Name:** "Nomad Collector"

**Duration:** June 1–August 31 (full summer)

**Why This Works:** Incentivizes travel and exploration. Appeals to vacationing collectors and road-trippers. Multi-sale-type inclusive (sales exist everywhere).

---

### Micro-Event 4: VINTAGE FASHION FESTIVAL

**Event Name:** Vintage Threads Festival

**Copy Line:** "Style through the ages. Find clothing, accessories, and wearables that turn heads and tell stories. Fashion is timeless."

**Badge Name:** "Wardrobe Curator"

**Duration:** August 15–September 15

**Why This Works:** Seasonal (back-to-school); appeal to fashion lovers. Works across all sale types (clothing everywhere). Emphasizes personal style over trends.

### Micro-Event 5: HOLIDAY GIFT HUNT

**Event Name:** Holiday Gift Hunt

**Copy Line:** "Thoughtful gifts, zero guilt. Find one-of-a-kind presents for everyone on your list—sustainable, affordable, and impossible to regret."

**Badge Name:** "Generous Finder"

**Duration:** October 15–December 24

**Why This Works:** Tied to real user intent (holiday shopping). Emphasizes gift-giving as a form of collecting/curation. Works across all sale types year-round; peaks in fall/winter.

---

### Micro-Event 6: NEW YEAR COLLECTION RESET

**Event Name:** New Year, New Collections

**Copy Line:** "Start fresh. Begin a new collection, deepen an existing passion, or chase a category you've never hunted before. Tradition starts with a single find."

**Badge Name:** "Resolution Seeker"

**Duration:** December 26–January 31

**Why This Works:** Ties to New Year's resolutions. Encourages trying new categories. Capitalizes on post-holiday sales surge and gift-card spending.

## Part 6: Organizer Track — Host-Facing Gamification

Organizers are **hosts and curators**, not hunters. Their gamification track emphasizes hospitality, reputation, and operational excellence. Different terminology, complementary philosophy.

---

### Organizer Rank Names (5 Tiers)

The organizer track mirrors shopper ranks in structure but with host-facing terminology.

#### Tier 1: **HOST**
- **Description:** You've opened your marketplace to the community. A host is the keeper of a sale.
- **Unlock:** First sale published and attended

#### Tier 2: **CURATOR**
- **Description:** You curate experiences. Your sales are organized, your items carefully presented, and your presentation shows intentionality.
- **Unlock:** 5 sales published + high shopper feedback ratings (4.5+ avg)

#### Tier 3: **MAESTRO**
- **Description:** Your sales run with precision. Inventory is robust, pricing is confident, and shoppers return because they trust your taste.
- **Unlock:** 15 sales + 1,000+ items sold + sustained 4.7+ rating + community reputation

#### Tier 4: **LUMINARY**
- **Description:** Your sales are destinations. Collectors travel specifically to attend. Your insights shape the secondary market in your region.
- **Unlock:** 30 sales + 5,000+ items sold + established brand + shopper community recognition

#### Tier 5: **LEGEND**
- **Description:** You are a pillar of the secondary market. Your reputation precedes you. Your sales are events. Your impact shapes how the community collects.
- **Unlock:** 50+ sales + 15,000+ items sold + top 1% organizer rating + established regional brand + mentoring newer organizers

---

### Organizer Copy Principles

**What differs from shopper copy:**

1. **Operational Language:** Use "operations," "efficiency," "capacity," "inventory management," "shopper experience."
2. **Hospitality Tone:** Emphasize that organizers are serving a community, not just processing transactions.
3. **Reputation Focus:** Highlight that ratings and reviews are currency. Build trust, earn loyalty.
4. **Professionalism:** Avoid gamey language. Badges feel earned, not cute.

**Organizer Voice Adjectives:**
- **Reliable** — Operations run smoothly; shoppers know what to expect
- **Respectful** — Treat collections and shoppers with care
- **Resourceful** — Solve problems, optimize space, maximize value
- **Visionary** — See potential in every item; curate with intention
- **Trustworthy** — Build relationships with shoppers and community

**Organizer Copy Sounds Like / Doesn't Sound Like:**

✅ **Sounds like:** "Your reputation score is 4.8. Shoppers trust your eye and your process."

❌ **Doesn't sound like:** "Badge unlocked! +200 XP!"

✅ **Sounds like:** "This season, 12 repeat shoppers attended your sales. You're building a collector community."

❌ **Doesn't sound like:** "You got more followers!"

---

### Organizer Micro-Events (Parallel to Shopper Events)

Organizers get parallel "Hosting Challenges" during seasonal expeditions:

- **Spring:** "First-Sale Flawless" — New organizers publish their first sale with >50 items, clear photos, accurate pricing
- **Summer:** "High-Volume Hero" — Host 3+ sales in a single season; manage 500+ items
- **Fall:** "Community Champion" — Achieve 4.8+ rating; receive 10+ repeat shopper visits
- **Winter:** "Seasonal Scholar" — Specialize in a single category (holiday décor, winter clothing, home goods); attract expert shoppers

## Part 7: Implementation Notes & Copy Maintenance

### Guardrails for All Copy

1. **No Game Mechanic Jargon:** Avoid "XP," "points," "grinding," "levels" in user-facing copy. Use narrative language (ranks, expeditions, hunts, finds).
2. **No Pressure:** Never use FOMO ("Don't miss out!"), urgency ("Hurry!"), or artificial scarcity ("Limited time!") to drive engagement. Trust the intrinsic appeal of the hunt.
3. **No Comparative Language:** Avoid leaderboard-focused copy ("You're #47 on the leaderboard"). Emphasize personal achievement and community belonging.
4. **Inclusive Always:** Every reference to "sales" must work across estate sales, yard sales, auctions, flea markets, and consignment. When in doubt, test the language against all five.
5. **Collection Mindset:** Frame purchases and finds as investments in lasting collections, not consumption. "Added to your collection" > "purchased."

### Copy Location Checklist

These copy patterns should appear in:
- ✅ Badge unlock notifications (push + email)
- ✅ Rank advancement screens (UI modal)
- ✅ Seasonal expedition splash pages
- ✅ In-app messaging (nudges, encouragements, progress updates)
- ✅ Leaderboard / community profiles
- ✅ Weekly digest emails
- ✅ Collector Passport page
- ✅ Hunt Pass marketing
- ✅ Onboarding flows
- ✅ Help text and tooltips
- ✅ Agent prompts (Claude CLAUDE.md directives)

### Copy Updates & Maintenance

- Update micro-event copy 60 days before event launch (seasonal planning cycle)
- Review all gamification copy for estate-sale bias quarterly
- Archive old seasonal expedition copy in `claude_docs/archive/gamification/` for reuse/remixing in future years
- Seasonal copy can be remixed but never should feel identical to prior years

## Part 8: Appendix — Multi-Sale-Type Glossary

Use this when writing any new gamification copy:

| Term | Usage | Examples |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Hunt** | Primary term for visiting a sale | "Your next hunt awaits" / "25 hunts completed" |
| **Find** | Primary term for items discovered/purchased | "Your collection of 89 finds" / "Rare find alert" |
| **Sale** | Generic term covering all 5 types | Avoid naming types unless necessary |
| **Market** | Broad alternative to "sale" | "What's happening in your market?" |
| **Organizer** | Host/curator; neutral across types | "Organizer's Choice" / "Meet the organizer" |
| **Collector** | Identity term for hunters | "Join 2,400 collectors in your region" |
| **Hunter** | Active role in the gamification narrative | "Hunters with your taste" |
| **Treasure** | Emotional term for special finds | "Treasures added this week" |
| **Collection** | Ownership + pride term | "Your collection grows" |
| **Scout / Ranger / Sage** | Rank names; use instead of "Level 1/2/3" | "Scout of the Guild" |
| **Guild** | Brand name for community | "Welcome to the Guild" |
| **Expedition** | Seasonal event term | "Spring Awakening Expedition" |
| **Badge** | Achievement term (visible to community) | "Vintage Verifier badge earned" |
