- Steam accounts (game libraries worth hundreds to thousands)

- Airline miles (often significant value, rules about transferability)
- Hotel loyalty points
- Credit card rewards points
- PayPal/Venmo balances

**Digital Content & Social Media:**
- YouTube channels (with subscriber base + ad revenue)
- TikTok/Instagram accounts with followings
- Intellectual property (ebooks, stock photography, music licenses)
- Email lists (newsletters, business subscribers)

**Online Business Assets:**
- Shopify/WooCommerce stores
- SaaS subscriptions (customer data, recurring revenue)
- Niche websites (blogs, ecommerce sites)
- Amazon seller accounts (FBA inventory, reputation)

**What they sell:** Game accounts, gold, items, boosting services

**Fees:** 5–10% of transaction

### 1.3 Estate Admin Pain Points (Legal & Practical)

#### Access Barriers
- **Two-factor authentication:** Accounts locked to deceased's phone number or email
- **Lost recovery codes:** Passphrases, seed phrases lost or poorly documented
- **Biometric locks:** Fingerprint/Face ID unavailable
- **Platform rules:** Many exchanges require identity verification for account access (difficult for fiduciaries)

#### Legal Barriers
- **Terms of Service violations:** Most gaming platforms forbid account transfers; technically "selling a Steam account" violates TOS
- **Cryptocurrency exchanges:** Some permit fiduciary access with death certificate + probate documents + letters testamentary; others refuse outright
- **Court hesitation:** Courts haven't articulated clear rules on fiduciary conduct for digital assets
- **Tax liability:** Unclear when liquidation triggers taxable events; stepped-up cost basis application unclear for crypto

#### Practical Barriers
- **Volatility risk:** Estate must decide whether to hold (risky) or liquidate immediately (may hit market lows)
- **Valuation challenge:** What's a YouTube channel worth? What's a gaming account with unknown value?
- **No market visibility:** Estates don't know "What should this sell for?"

#### Emotional/Family Barriers
- **Heir attachment:** Family members may disagree on liquidation timing or method
- **Sentimental value:** Gaming accounts represent years of gameplay/progression

### 1.4 Market Signals

**Evidence of demand:**
- 90% of crypto holders concerned about inheritance planning (Estate Mentors survey)
- Growing market for "digital asset executors" and specialized lawyers
- Reddit threads (r/personalfinance, r/legaladvice) regularly ask "How do I inherit my dad's Bitcoin?" or "What happens to my Steam library when I die?"
- Established platforms (PlayerAuctions, G2G) thrive on gaming asset sales (proven market demand)
- Cryptocurrency estate planning guides proliferating (sign of market pain)

**Market size estimate:**
- Gaming assets alone: $70B+ global virtual goods market (2024)
- Cryptocurrency held: $2.5T+ total crypto holdings (many have no estate plan)
- Estate value captured: Unknown, but conservatively 1–3% of digital estate assets are ever liquidated (rest lost)

**Verdict:** Clear, underserved market. 90% of digital assets are abandoned when holders die (access lost = effective loss).

## Phase 3: Feasibility Verdicts

### Idea 1: Digital Asset Inventory Tab (MVP)
**Complexity:** Low
**Timeline:** 3–4 weeks
**Risk Level:** VERY LOW

**Build:**
- New organizer form section: "Digital Assets"
- Optional fields: Account type (Steam, Reddit, Crypto, etc.), Description, Value estimate
- Frontend: New "Digital Assets" tab on estate sale page (if any listed)
- No marketplace integration; just inventory

**Risks:**
- Liability (organizers claim ownership of accounts they don't have rights to)
  - Mitigation: Terms of Service disclaimer ("Listing doesn't guarantee right to sell")
- Low adoption (not all organizers have digital assets)

**ROI:** Low immediate ROI (feature visibility only). High future value if marketplace launches.

**Verdict:** **BUILD NOW (Phase 2)** — Defer to Q3 2026. Prerequisite for marketplace. Estimated effort: 2 weeks. Low effort, accumulates data for future marketplace.

---

### Idea 2: Digital Asset Valuation Tool
**Complexity:** Medium
**Timeline:** 6–8 weeks
**Risk Level:** LOW-MEDIUM

**Build:**
- Backend: API integrations (SteamDB, YouTube Data API, gaming marketplace APIs)
- Frontend: Quick valuation form (account type, metrics)
- Algorithm: Regression model (subscriber count → YouTube channel value, game library size → Steam account value)
- Data source: Historical sales data from PlayerAuctions, G2G (scraped/API where available)

**Risks:**
- Data quality (valuation models only as good as historical data)
- Rapid market shifts (gaming cosmetics prices fluctuate weekly)
- Legal liability (if valuation is grossly wrong, estate may have grounds to sue)
  - Mitigation: Clearly state "estimate only, not professional appraisal"

**ROI:** Useful as educational tool; increases perceived credibility of digital asset sales.

**Verdict:** **DEFER** — Medium effort, medium ROI. Revisit Q4 2026 after marketplace MVP. Estimated effort: 2 sprints.

---

### Idea 3: Gaming Account Marketplace Integration (via PlayerAuctions/G2G)
**Complexity:** Medium
**Timeline:** 8–10 weeks
**Risk Level:** MEDIUM

**Build:**
- Backend: API integration with PlayerAuctions (if they expose it) or manual listing relay
- Workflow: Organizer lists account on FindA.Sale → system syndicates to partner marketplace
- TOS review: Confirm that estate liquidation is exempt from "no account transfers" rule (likely not)
- Escrow: FindA.Sale holds funds, buyer confirms account access, release to organizer

**Risks:**
- **TOS violation:** Steam, Xbox, PlayStation explicitly forbid account sales. Listing may violate terms.
  - Mitigation: Consult legal. Only offer for "games that allow transfers" (RuneScape, EVE Online, etc.)
- **API availability:** Partner marketplaces may not expose APIs for third-party syndication
- **Fraud:** Buyer pays, account gets stolen back by original account owner → massive liability
  - Mitigation: Require proof of death certificate + estate documentation

**Market impact:** If launched successfully, huge upside. If one fraud case → legal disaster.

**Verdict:** **REJECT (for now)** — Legal risk too high without formal counsel review. Recommended: Consult IP/tech law firm ($5–$10K) before proceeding. If legal gives green light, revisit Q4 2026.

### Idea 4: Cryptocurrency Wallet Recovery Partnership
**Complexity:** Very High
**Timeline:** 12–16 weeks (partnership + integration)
**Risk Level:** HIGH

**Build:**
- Partner with Ledger Recover, Unchained Capital, or similar
- Workflow: Estate admin describes lost crypto → FindA.Sale facilitates recovery
- Integration: API to partner, secure credential passing, escrow for recovery fees
- Support: Educational content, recovery guide

**Risks:**
- **Regulatory risk:** Cryptocurrency regulations still evolving; partnership could become non-viable if regulations tighten
- **Liability:** If recovery fails, estate may sue for lost crypto
- **Operational complexity:** Each case is unique; requires specialized human support
- **Reputational risk:** Crypto market volatility means publicizing this feature may attract scammers

**Verdict:** **REJECT** — Too risky for current stage. Revisit 2027 Q2+ after regulatory clarity improves and FindA.Sale has dedicated legal/ops team.
