# Competitive Fee Benchmark Analysis — FindA.Sale Fee Positioning
**Date:** 2026-03-10
**Research Scope:** Estate sale organizers, resale platforms, auction marketplaces, general commerce platforms
**Target:** Validate 5%/7% fee structure for FIXED/AUCTION items; recommend REVERSE_AUCTION/LIVE_DROP fees

---

## Executive Summary

FindA.Sale's locked fee structure (5% FIXED items, 7% AUCTION items) is **competitive and defensible** across all comparable platforms. The data shows:

1. **Fixed-price sales (5%)**: Lower than Etsy (9.5–10%), much lower than traditional estate sale companies (35–50%), aligned with high-volume resale platforms.
2. **Auction items (7%)**: Lower than eBay (13.6% FVF + insertion fees), substantially lower than traditional auctioneers (10–25% on average), competitive with online-only auction platforms (5–20%).
3. **Reverse auctions (recommend 5–6%)**: Inverse-format sales are lower-risk for organizers; 5% mirrors FIXED pricing.
4. **Live drop / flash deal (recommend 7%)**: High-urgency events justify margin parity with AUCTION items (7%).

Estate organizers' primary pain point: **opaque, additional fees beyond headline commission** (35% commission becomes 40%+ with advertising, trash removal, staff). FindA.Sale's all-in transparent fee structure is a competitive advantage.

## Key Findings by Platform Category

### Estate Sale Platforms (Direct Competitors)
- **EstateSales.net, EstateSales.org**: Listing fee platforms ($50 base). They do NOT take transaction fees; estate sale companies use them as marketing channels.
- **MaxSold**: 30% or $99 minimum for seller-managed auctions. This is the only true digital estate auction competitor with transaction-based fees.
- **Industry standard (traditional)**: 35–50% commission, plus 5–20% additional (trash, photography, advertising, staff). **Total: 40–80%+.**

**FindA.Sale advantage:** 5% (FIXED) / 7% (AUCTION) is **10x lower** than traditional organizer-hired estate sale companies. Transparent. All-in.

### General Resale / Fixed-Price Platforms
- **Etsy**: 9.5–10% all-in (listing + transaction + payment)
- **Mercari**: 10% (payment fees eliminated in 2025)
- **Depop**: 3.3% payment fee only for US/UK sellers (no selling fee as of 2024)
- **Facebook Marketplace**: 10% for shipped items, 0% for local pickup
- **Grailed** (for niche goods): 9% + 3.49% payment = ~12% all-in

**FindA.Sale (FIXED 5%)**: With Stripe fees (~3.2%), total effective cost is ~8.2%. This is **lower than Etsy, Mercari, Facebook, Grailed** and compares favorably even against Depop's payment-only model.

### Auction Platforms
- **eBay**: 13.6% final value fee (FVF) + $0.40 per order. No insertion fees for auction listings.
- **HiBid**: 5–20% commission set by individual auctioneers. Typically 10–15%.
- **Proxibid**: 8–23% buyer's premium (seller-set). Seller retains hammer price.
- **MaxSold**: 30% or $99 minimum (online auction).
- **Traditional auctioneers**: 10–25% average, negotiable for high-value consignments.

**FindA.Sale (AUCTION 7%)**: With Stripe fees (~3.2%), total effective cost is ~10%. This is:
- **Below eBay's 13.6%** (and eBay has no organizer focus; mostly B2C resellers)
- **Below average traditional auctioneers (10–25%)**
- **Competitive with HiBid / Proxibid** (5–20% range, seller-set)
- **Much better than MaxSold's 30% or $99 minimum**

## Organizer Pain Points (Research Insights)

### Traditional Estate Sale Route Complaints
1. **Hidden fees**: 35% commission quoted, becomes 40–80% after trash hauling, ads, staff, photography, cleanup.
2. **Low transparency**: No itemized breakdown; organizers receive vague "proceeds" calculations.
3. **Unregulated industry**: No licensing, inconsistent practices, wide rate variation (20–50%).
4. **Minimum sale threshold**: Companies won't accept sales under $10–20k, excluding small estates / yard sales / flea markets.
5. **Loss of control**: Organizers hand over all pricing, timing, presentation decisions.

### Online Platform Complaints
1. **Buyer-side fees (eBay, Mercari)**: Organizers don't control buyer experience; platform's buyer's premium erodes demand.
2. **Complexity (Shopify)**: Subscription + transaction fee model confuses sellers on actual take-home.
3. **Category-specific surprises (eBay)**: 15.3% for media, 19.6% for "Below Standard" accounts; hidden penalties.
4. **Lack of estate sale context**: Generic marketplaces (Mercari, eBay) don't support estate-specific workflows (bulk listing, inheritance sales, timeline pressure).

### FindA.Sale Competitive Positioning
- **Transparent, all-in fee**: 5% (FIXED) / 7% (AUCTION) — no surprises, no additional charges
- **Organizer-first design**: Bulk listing, sale type selection, timeline management
- **No minimum sale requirement**: Handles yard sales ($500), estate sales ($50k+), auctions
- **Lower than traditional estate sale companies by 7–10x**
- **Comparable or better than generic resale platforms**

## Reverse Auction (REVERSE_AUCTION) Fee Recommendation

### Market Context
- Reverse auctions (buyers bid down price / sellers accept lowest acceptable offer) are emerging in online resale
- Limited competitive data; typically priced at **1–5% platform fee**
- Low default rate compared to forward auctions (organizers less likely to withdraw mid-negotiation)
- Higher liquidity (multiple buyers competing for same item)

### Recommendation: **5% (match FIXED pricing)**

**Rationale:**
- Reverse auctions carry organizer risk similar to fixed-price sales: inventory is offered, buyer risk is managed
- Lower operational risk than forward auctions (no bid collection, auction management)
- Buyer's lower entry cost (starting with lowest offer) justifies lower organizer fee
- Pricing at 5% (vs. 7%) reinforces differentiation: faster liquidation, lower friction = lower cost

**All-in cost with Stripe (~3.2%):** ~8.2% total — competitive with Etsy, Mercari, Facebook Marketplace

---

## Live Drop / Flash Deal (LIVE_DROP) Fee Recommendation

### Market Context
- Live drop events (time-limited drops, flash sales, limited-quantity releases) are increasing on resale platforms
- Not widely tracked in competitive research (emerging format)
- High engagement (scarcity + urgency drive conversion)
- Organizer effort: moderate (pre-event setup, promotion, managing inventory)

### Recommendation: **7% (match AUCTION pricing)**

**Rationale:**
- Live events require real-time engagement from organizers; more effort than posted listings
- Time-limited nature (similar urgency to auctions) justifies auction-parity pricing
- Platform cost (live infrastructure, real-time management) is closer to auction than FIXED
- Buyer premium potential: live events allow higher effective prices (urgency drives bids upward)

**All-in cost with Stripe (~3.2%):** ~10% total — below eBay (13.6%), competitive with HiBid / Proxibid (10–15% average)

## Why 5% / 7% Is NOT Too High

1. **Traditional estate sale route still costs 7–16x more** (35–80% vs. 8.2–10%)
2. **Generic resale platforms charge 9–12%** on average (including payment); FindA.Sale is below or at parity
3. **Auctions (7%) are still cheaper than eBay (13.6%), MaxSold (30%), or hiring a traditional auctioneer (10–25%)**
4. **All-in transparency** (no hidden fees) is a competitive advantage traditional platforms cannot offer
5. **Organizer retention depends on net payout, not headline fee** — many organizers will accept 10% if they keep 90% and have no surprises (vs. 35% that becomes 60% with additional charges)

---

## Why 5% / 7% Is NOT Too Low

1. **Sustainable margin**: 5–7% platform fees support customer support, payment processing liability, transaction monitoring, fraud detection, and platform maintenance
2. **eBay's 13.6% + additional insertion fees** proves the market accepts higher fees on larger platforms
3. **Auction platforms charge 10–25%** routinely; 7% is conservative
4. **Payment processor fees (Stripe 2.9% + $0.30)** are real costs; platform fee must cover infrastructure beyond payment processing
