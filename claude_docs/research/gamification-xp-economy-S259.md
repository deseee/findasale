## 1. Seasonal Reset – Game Design Verdict

### What Successful Games Actually Do

The consensus across competitive and live-service games is **soft reset over hard reset**:

- **Fortnite:** [Account Level persists](https://www.playbite.com/does-your-level-reset-every-season-in-fortnite/). Season Level resets to 1, Battle Pass expires. XP carries over. Cosmetics stay forever. Only seasonal *challenges* expire.
- **Apex Legends:** [1.5-tier soft reset per split](https://help.ea.com/en/articles/apex-legends/ranked/). Diamond players drop to Platinum, Platinum to Gold. Two resets per season (every 6 weeks). No hard reset.
- **Rocket League:** [Placement-based soft reset](https://www.rocketleague.com/en/news/ranked-season---how-it-works-and-how-it-will-evolve/). Your MMR guides placement matches (×10), which double-weight progression (+16 MMR vs. +9 normal).
- **League of Legends:** [Tier-based soft reset](https://support-leagueoflegends.riotgames.com/hc/en-us/articles/4406003617555-Ranked-Years-Seasons-and-End-of-Season-Rewards/), plus Honor requirement for seasonal rewards. All permanent progression (skins, champs) persists.
- **Path of Exile:** [Full reset with permanent escape hatch](https://www.poewiki.net/wiki/League). Characters move to Standard League (permanent) after season ends. Players *choose* hard reset each 3–4 months because fresh economy = fair competition and opportunity.
- **Clash Royale:** [Soft reset into 10–12K trophy range](https://royalezone.com/blogs/clash-royale-trophy-reset-2025/). Not everyone drops to the same point. Players who hit 15K reset to 12K. Below 10K is permanent gate (no reset).

### Why Path of Exile's Hard Reset Works (Yet Most Games Don't)

Path of Exile players *voluntarily embrace* hard reset because:
1. [Fresh economy = valuable drops](https://mobalytics.gg/poe-2/guides/leagues/). Day 1 items are worth gold; week 4 items are worth nothing. Rush meta creates hype.
2. Safe harbor exists (Standard League). You never *lose* progress—you archive it.
3. New mechanics drop with each league. Reset = mandatory rebalancing + excitement.

**Other games (Fortnite, LoL, CoD) avoid hard reset because:**
- Cosmetics/identity are permanent (buying cosmetics = long-term commitment)
- Casual players resent starting at zero
- Secondary markets exist—resetting inventory breaks them

### FindA.Sale Seasonal Reset Recommendation

**SOFT RESET with archive tier:**

1. **Annual (January):** Tier 1 resets to Copper, Tier 2+ resets to Bronze, Tier 3+ resets to Silver. Grandmaster uncapped (no reset). Hunt Pass progress carries over for uncapped tiers.
2. **What persists:** Collector badges, seasonal challenge completions, collection/passport entries, lifetime XP (if tracking), profile reputation.
3. **Archive:** End-of-season "Seasonal Collector" badge showing peak tier reached (e.g., "Peak Emerald – Winter 2025"). Organizers see your history.
4. **Reasoning:** Estate sale shoppers are *inherently seasonal*. Winter/spring slump is real. Reset resets the local market climb without erasing identity. Shoppers who don't play for 6 months can jump back in without feeling permanently behind.

## 2. Daily Engagement Mechanics

### Top 3 Game Design Patterns

#### Pattern A: Streak Mechanics with Loss Aversion
**Duolingo model:** [7-day streak = 3.6× completion likelihood](https://blog.duolingo.com/how-duolingo-streak-builds-habit/). [60% widget uptick when streaks are visible](https://www.choicehacking.com/2023/05/25/how-duolingo-used-psychology-to-make-learning-addictive/).

**Psychology:** Humans fear loss more than seeking gain (loss aversion). Once you've earned 20 days, you don't want to lose it. Notifications say "Your 20-day streak is at risk!" not "Earn 1 more day!"

**Forgiving design:** Streak freezes let you miss a day without breaking momentum. Humane friction.

#### Pattern B: Escalating Rewards (Daily → 7-Day → 30-Day Combo)
**Pokemon GO model:** [First catch/spin of day = 500 XP + 600 Stardust](https://pokemongohub.net/post/wiki/daily-weekly-streaks/). [7-day streak = 2500 XP + 3000 Stardust (5× daily)](https://niantic.helpshift.com/hc/en-6-pokemon-go/faq/105-daily-bonuses/). Streak resets and loop again.

**Mechanics:**
- Day 1: +500 XP
- Day 2: +500 XP
- ...
- Day 7: +2500 XP (bonus unlocks)
- Day 8: resets to +500 XP (fresh loop, but player just earned prestige)

**Addiction loop:** Diminishing returns prevent burnout (same reward every day = boring), while escalation creates "I'm almost at the jackpot" momentum.

#### Pattern C: Appointment Mechanics + Limited-Time Quests
**Fortnite model:** [Weekly quests arrive Thursday; they *expire* after one week](https://www.dexerto.com/fortnite/all-fortnite-weekly-challenges-amp-quests-2741946/). [Daily challenges stack to max 3](https://fortnite.fandom.com/wiki/Quests).

**Why it works:** Creates urgency without punishment. You can't hoard quests. FOMO (fear of missing out) drives consistent logins. But it's not a streak (missing one day ≠ losing 20 days).

**Danger:** [Player backlash when quests expire permanently](https://www.resetera.com/threads/kotaku-fortnite%E2%80%9s-new-weekly-quest-system-sucks-even-with-changes.677077/). Balance is critical.

---

### FindA.Sale Daily Engagement Design

**Hybrid model combining all three:**

#### Daily Login Bonus (Simple anchor)
- Opens the app 1x/day → 10 XP + 1 Explorer Point (cosmetic currency for badges)
- No payment required. Just open. No streak at this level.

#### Weekly Streak (Loss Aversion Core)
- Complete *any* XP-earning action 3+ days in a week → "Weekly Active" badge + 25 XP bonus unlock
- Resets Mondays. If you miss a week, you start fresh. No cascading guilt.
- Notification: "You've earned 2/3 days this week! One more for Weekly Active!" (positive frame)

#### 30-Day Prestige Tracker (Escalation)
- Track consecutive months of "Weekly Active" completions
- Month 1: Pathfinder badge
- Month 2: Scout badge
- Month 3: Ranger badge (now you're visibly different in leaderboards)
- Resets monthly. But your badge history stays (e.g., "Ranger (3 months)" displays on profile)

#### Seasonal Mini-Quests (Appointment Mechanics)
- 5 quests per week, rotate Thursdays at 9 AM PT
- Examples: "Visit 2 sales this week" (15 XP) | "Leave 1 review" (10 XP) | "Find an item on your wishlist" (20 XP)
- Can complete multiple weeks' quests, but new ones always arrive (no hoarding)
- Old quests archive to your Loot Log (bragging rights)

**Expected behavior:** Casual shoppers visit 2–3x/week. This design ensures they hit "Weekly Active" without grinding, while engaged collectors can stack for "Ranger" prestige.

## 3. Sage Tier – Alternative Big Payoffs (No Organizer Coercion)

Patrick's constraint: **Sage must NOT require organizers to do anything special for specific buyers.**

Presale access is off the table.

### Option A: Sage Collector's Passport – Exclusive Completion Rewards
**Concept:** Sage-tier shoppers unlock a hidden "Collector's Passport" with region-specific sections (Grand Rapids neighborhoods, antique categories, etc.). Completing sections earns XP + unique badges.

**Big Payoff:** When you finish a region, you get a shareable trophy: "I collected the Grand Rapids Heritage Passport!" (with map graphic). Share to Instagram → potential new users see it → download FindA.Sale.

**Why it works:**
- No organizer participation
- Feels like an achievement (trophy = social proof)
- Virality: collector identity becomes public
- Example: "Antique Furniture Collector (Passau-Level)" displays on profile
- Organizers see prestige in their comments but don't "enable" it

**Virality potential:** Medium-high. Collectors talk about collections. Passport completion is Instagram-worthy.

---

### Option B: Sage Treasure Trail – Gamified Discovery Feed
**Concept:** Sage-tier unlock a "Treasure Trail" system where:
- Organizer posts items normally
- FindA.Sale AI tags them with discovery categories (e.g., "Rare Midcentury," "Under $20," "Local Maker")
- Sage shoppers see a personalized map: "5 treasure items in downtown Grand Rapids this week"
- Finding and documenting the item (photo upload + metadata) = treasure logged

**Big Payoff:** "Treasure Master" leaderboard (top 10 Sages by items logged). Monthly winner gets:
- Featured badge
- "Treasure Master – March 2026" role on their profile
- Early notification of next month's sales (algorithm boost, not organizer manual action)

**Why it works:**
- Gamifies hunting (already natural to estate sale shoppers)
- Creates user-generated content (photos)
- Organizers benefit from tagged items without effort
- Prestige is algorithmic (not dependent on organizer action)

**Virality potential:** High. Treasure hunting is shareable. "Just found the rarest piece at [sale]!" → photo → TikTok/Instagram.

---

### Option C: Sage Loot Legend – Lifetime Collection Registry
**Concept:** Sage unlock a personal "Loot Legend" profile page showing:
- All items ever purchased on FindA.Sale (with photos from loot log)
- Total spend, total items, favorite categories
- "Collection journey" timeline showing acquisition dates and stories
- Public profile URL (shareable, portfolio-like)

**Big Payoff:** Sage-exclusive leaderboards by category:
- "Antiques Collector – Leather Bound Books" (rank #3 nationally)
- "Vintage Fashion Curator – Spring 2026" (rank #1 Grand Rapids)
- Monthly featured collectors on FindA.Sale homepage

**Why it works:**
- Taps into collector identity (these shoppers love showing off finds)
- Profile becomes portfolio (tangible status)
- Organizers benefit from collectors' credibility on reviews
- No organizer action required

**Virality potential:** Very high. Collectors *want* to share collections. "Check out my Loot Legend—1,200+ pieces over 3 years" = Pinterest-level shareable.

---

### **Recommendation Ranking:**

1. **Option C (Loot Legend) – HIGHEST impact.** Collectors' identity + portfolio + leaderboard prestige hits all three needs (status, discovery, community). Easiest to build on existing loot log.
2. **Option B (Treasure Trail) – HIGH engagement loop.** Creates weekly appointments + UGC. Organizers naturally benefit.
3. **Option A (Passport) – Medium engagement.** Cleaner than B, less viral than C. Good secondary goal.

**Suggestion:** Implement **C as primary Sage perk**, then add **B as secondary monthly rotation** to keep engagement fresh.

## 5. Dollar-Tied XP – Recommendation

### Research: How Others Do It

**Starbucks Rewards (tiered, dollar-tied):**
- [Green tier: 1 Star per $1 spent](https://www.openloyalty.io/insider/starbucks-rewards-program)
- [Gold tier: 1.2 Stars per $1 spent](https://www.openloyalty.io/insider/starbucks-rewards-program)
- [Reserve tier: 1.7 Stars per $1 spent](https://www.openloyalty.io/insider/starbucks-rewards-program)
- **Climbing tiers rewards spending.** Money → Progression.

**Airline miles & credit cards:** Always dollar-tied. Spend $1 = earn 1 mile. Incentivizes high-value customers.

### Pros & Cons for FindA.Sale

**Pro (dollar-tied):**
- Revenue signal: shoppers who spend $500 are clearly engaged
- Encourages larger hauls (higher AOV)
- Aligns business incentive (gross margin grows)

**Con (dollar-tied):**
- Estate sale shoppers are *bargain hunters*. Average purchase: $10–40 per item.
- Estate sale *volume* matters more than per-item spend. Someone buying 10 × $5 items has higher intent than 1 × $50 item.
- "Pay-to-win" perception. Casual browsers (no money) feel left out.
- Wealthy outliers game system (one rich collector >> 100 regular shoppers).

### FindA.Sale Recommendation: **Hybrid (Flat + Bonus)**

**Primary: Flat per-item (not dollar-tied)**
- Purchase any item, any price: 15 XP
- Removes friction. $5 brooch = 15 XP, same as $500 furniture piece.
- Aligns with estate sale culture (quantity, discovery, hunting).

**Secondary: Bonus tier for larger orders**
- Spend $50+ in one sale/day: additional 25 XP
- Spend $100+: additional 50 XP
- Caps at 1x/day to prevent gaming (buying multiple $50 carts same day = no stacking)
- Incentivizes "haul" shopping without punishing small buys.

**Rationale:**
- Estate sale shoppers shop for the *experience* (hunt, discovery, community), not status symbols.
- Flat per-item rewards the core behavior (finding unique items).
- Bonus encourages cart growth without creating "pay to win" perception.
- Hunt Pass multiplier sits *on top* of both, so revenue customers still get accelerated rewards.
