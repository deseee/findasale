# Brand Voice Guide – FindA.Sale

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-16 (Rewritten to reflect full market scope)

---

## Mission & Positioning

**What we do:**
FindA.Sale is a platform that helps organizers of ALL types of community resale events run sales efficiently and helps shoppers discover the secondhand treasures they love.

**Who we serve:**
- **Organizers:** Estate sale companies, garage sale hosts, auctioneers (live and online), flea market operators, tag sale organizers, rummage sale coordinators, and anyone running a community resale event
- **Shoppers:** Treasure hunters, collectors, bargain seekers, thrift enthusiasts, and anyone who loves the hunt for secondhand finds

**Our north star:** "Find All The Sales" — a platform that celebrates ALL forms of community resale, from formal estate liquidations to a family's weekend garage sale. We reduce manual work for organizers and enable shoppers to discover everything from rare collectibles to hidden gems in their neighborhood.

---

## Brand Personality

FindA.Sale feels like a trusted local partner who speaks plainly and gets the job done. Not corporate, not stuffy. Professional but warm. We celebrate the community and joy of resale — whether you're liquidating a multi-building estate, hosting a backyard garage sale, or running an auction. We're here to make both sides feel supported.

### Core Attributes
- **Helpful** — We solve real problems for event organizers of all types. No jargon. Straight talk.
- **Honest** — Transparent about fees, process, and limitations.
- **Inclusive** — Community resale comes in many forms. All are welcome and celebrated.
- **Practical** — We focus on what works, not what sounds good.
- **Curious** — We love the hunt. Treasure takes many forms.

## Tone Across Contexts

### Organizer-Facing Copy
When speaking to organizers, we acknowledge the unique needs of each event type. Estate sales involve emotional complexity and grief. Garage sales are family events filled with nostalgia. Auctions require efficiency. Flea markets demand presentation. We're the capable partner who understands YOUR specific context.

**Tone markers:**
- Respectful of the work (whether it's a living or a one-time event)
- Solution-focused ("Here's how we help")
- Confidence-building ("You've got this")
- Professional but accessible
- Minimize jargon
- Celebrate their expertise (they know their items; we help them reach buyers)

**Examples:**
> "Set up a professional estate sale in minutes. We'll handle online reach; you focus on the sale itself."

> "Running a garage sale? Get serious local buyers without the legwork of spreading the word."

> "Your auction deserves the right bidders. List once, reach thousands across your region."

### Shopper-Facing Copy
When speaking to shoppers, we celebrate the hunt. Whether it's a formal estate sale, a neighborhood garage sale, an online auction, or a flea market — the experience is adventure, discovery, and community.

**Tone markers:**
- Enthusiastic but not forced
- Focus on the experience (discovery, community, deals, adventure)
- Inclusive (everyone belongs here, no matter their budget or interest)
- Encouraging exploration
- Friendly and conversational
- Celebratory about all types of secondhand treasures

**Examples:**
> "Find your next favorite treasure. Browse upcoming sales, auctions, flea markets, and garage sales all in one place."

> "Every weekend is a new adventure. Chat with organizers, explore categories, discover what you didn't know you were looking for."

> "From rare collectibles to everyday bargains. All types of secondhand events. All near you."

## Writing Conventions

### Headline Language
- Active voice, strong verbs
- Benefit-forward ("Find," "Discover," "Unlock," "Connect," "Explore")
- Short and punchy (under 10 words ideal)
- Inclusive of all event types (when relevant)
- Examples: "Find All The Sales," "Discover Your Next Treasure," "Connect Buyers & Events," "Explore Sales Near You"

### Body Copy
- Conversational, second-person ("you")
- Short sentences and paragraphs
- One idea per sentence
- Avoid corporate speak and jargon
- Use contractions ("we're," "don't," "it's")
- Acknowledge the breadth of resale (estate sales, garage sales, auctions, etc.) naturally — don't just add parenthetical lists

### Do's
- ✅ Use concrete, varied examples (reference different types of sales and events)
- ✅ Be specific about benefits across organizer types
- ✅ Acknowledge real user needs (efficiency, reach, joy of discovery)
- ✅ Celebrate curiosity, community, and sustainable resale
- ✅ Show confidence in the product
- ✅ Weave all sale types naturally into messaging

### Don'ts
- ❌ Avoid industry jargon (liquidation, estate management, consignment terms, etc.)
- ❌ Don't treat estate sales as the "default" with others as afterthoughts
- ❌ Don't be overly casual (no slang, no "bro" speak)
- ❌ Don't use clichés ("game-changer," "disrupting," etc.)
- ❌ Don't promise more than we deliver
- ❌ Don't minimize the emotional weight of estate sales or liquidation
- ❌ Don't make garage sales or casual events feel "lesser"

## Brand Guardrails

### What We Are NOT
- A classifieds board (we're a curated marketplace focused on secondhand community events)
- Stuffy or corporate
- Focused on high-end-only (treasure comes in all price points)
- Exclusive or gatekeeping
- Estate-sale-centric (we celebrate all forms of community resale)

### What We Celebrate
- Local commerce and neighborhood connections
- All forms of community resale (formal and casual)
- Treasure hunting and discovery across all price points
- Sustainability through secondhand commerce
- The expertise of organizers (professionals and first-timers)
- The joy of finding something unexpected
- Community over corporate

## Visual Tone Notes (For Design & Video)

- Photography should feel authentic, not stock. Real items, real organizers, real shoppers — across all event types.
- Show the variety: formal estate sales alongside casual garage sales. Online auctions alongside flea markets.
- Color palette: Warm and inviting. Not clinical or corporate. Approachable.
- Typography: Clear and friendly. Prioritize readability.
- Imagery: Celebrate the objects. Show human moments (excitement, discovery, community, connection).
- No gatekeeping. Everyone is welcome — whether you're liquidating a 5-building estate or a backyard shed.

## Tone Consistency Checklist

When creating new copy or reviewing existing messaging:

- [ ] Does this celebrate ALL forms of community resale, or just estates?
- [ ] Is the tone warm and inclusive (not corporate or stuffy)?
- [ ] Are concrete benefits stated (not vague promises)?
- [ ] Is the language conversational and jargon-free?
- [ ] Does it acknowledge the specific context for organizers?
- [ ] Does it celebrate discovery and community for shoppers?
- [ ] Is "Find All The Sales" embedded as the north star?
