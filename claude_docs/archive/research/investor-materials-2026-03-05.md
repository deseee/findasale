## Executive Summary (1-Page)

**FindA.Sale** is a mobile-first estate sale marketplace for the $4B US estate sale industry — a massive, underserved market with incumbents (EstateSales.net, 7.1M visits/month) consistently rated 1.4 stars on Trustpilot. We give estate sale organizers an AI-powered, shopper-friendly platform to list, manage, and monetize sales with a transparent 5% fee — versus competitors charging 13–20%.

**The problem:** Baby boomers (78 million, ages 60–78) are downsizing at historic rates. Estate sale organizers need digital tools to handle photo cataloging, online sales, virtual queuing, auctions, and shopper communication. Existing platforms are stuck in 2005: bad mobile UX, opaque fees, no AI support, and 1.4-star customer service.

**Our solution:** A production-deployed PWA built in 14 months featuring AI-powered item tagging, real-time auctions with Socket.io, instant Stripe Connect payouts, a virtual line queue, push notifications, shopper messaging, and 20+ organizer workflow tools. No listing fees. 5% platform fee only on completed transactions.

**Traction:** Production MVP live at finda.sale. 21 development phases complete including Stripe payments, AI tagging, live bidding, and affiliate system. Beta recruitment begins Q1 2026 targeting Grand Rapids, MI organizer community.

**Market:** $4B domestic estate sale market + $186B secondhand economy, growing 17% annually. Structural tailwind: 150,000–200,000 US estate sales/year, average $18,000–$20,000 GMV per sale.

**Business model:** 5% platform fee on fixed-price sales, 7% on auctions. Zero listing fees. AI tagging add-on post-beta. Path to subscription tiers for high-volume organizers.

**Ask:** Seed funding of $150,000–$300,000 to fund beta organizer acquisition (5–20 organizers, 2 markets), AI infrastructure, full-time technical support, and working capital.

**Team:** Patrick DeSee, founder. Solo technical build leveraging AI-assisted development (Claude). Grand Rapids, MI local market expertise.

## Financial Model — 3-Year Projection

### Assumptions
- Avg GMV per sale: $5,000 (conservative — avg estate sale is $15K–$20K; online component ~25–35% initially)
- Platform fee: 5% fixed, 7% auction (blended 5.5%)
- Stripe processing cost: ~2.2% of GMV (net after Stripe fee structure)
- Monthly infrastructure costs: $200–400 (Vercel + Railway + Neon at scale)
- AI tagging cost: ~$0.03/item → $15/month at 500 items/month

*Net revenue after Stripe fees and infrastructure costs

### Break-Even
- Monthly infrastructure: ~$300 at beta scale
- **Break-even: 3–4 active organizers running 1 sale/month each** (achievable by end of beta)

### Key Sensitivities
- GMV per sale: every $1K increase in average sale size adds ~$55/sale in revenue
- Organizer churn: keeping organizer retention above 70% is the primary business risk
- Auction mix: if 30% of items go to auction, blended fee rises to 5.6%, improving margins
