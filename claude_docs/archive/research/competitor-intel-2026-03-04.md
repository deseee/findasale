# Competitor Intelligence — FindA.Sale

Based on deep research across Trustpilot, BBB, Reddit, SiteJabber, Facebook groups, and app store reviews.
Generated 2026-03-04 (ROADMAP v2 deep research). Load only during strategic planning sessions.

---

## estatesales.net (Primary — 7.1M visits/month)

**Strengths:** Massive shopper traffic, brand recognition, map + location search, large directory.

**Organizer pain points:** Customer service virtually nonexistent (1.4 stars Trustpilot). Opaque fees ($99 listing + $2.95/item not communicated upfront). Broken seller navigation. Support staff reportedly told organizers "I don't care."

**Shopper pain points:** Terrible photo coverage (closeups without room views). Item misrepresentation. Dysfunctional auction bidding. No ability to save/organize favorites by category.

**#1 wish:** Better support + pricing transparency. Shoppers want room overview photos.

## EstateSales.org (Secondary — 950K visits/month)

Lower fees ($50 base), more responsive support, better for online auctions. Weaker traffic.

## Facebook Marketplace

Algorithm flags "estate" as Fair Housing Act violation. Ads with "auction"/"bid" get compliance blocks. Low effectiveness. Direct stranger contact is safety risk. Organizers describe it as "emotional, time-consuming."

## Craigslist

Outdated, risky. Rampant scams, zero security, declining legitimate buyers.

## Yard Sale Treasure Map (4.7 stars App Store)

Best-in-class route planning. Complaints: broken Google Maps, many listed sales don't happen, paywall for day filtering.

## LiveAuctioneers (2.1 stars, 332 reviews)

Reports of fraud, high shipping costs, no buyer advocacy. HiBid has better reputation.

## FindA.Sale Advantages to Exploit

Responsive support, photo guidance for organizers (room overviews + AI), smarter search/favorites, transparent pricing, pricing guidance tools, modern mobile-first UX.
