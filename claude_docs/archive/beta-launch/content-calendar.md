## Week 3: Education & Trust-Building

### Day 15 (Monday): "What Makes a Great Estate Sale Listing?" Blog Post

**Platform:** Blog + Email (send to organizer list)
**Format:** Blog post (400–500 words)
**Audience:** Estate sale organizers (build credibility + practical value)

#### Blog Post:

**Title:** "What Makes a Great Estate Sale Listing? (And How AI Can Help)"

```
A great estate sale listing doesn't just describe items. It tells a story. It helps shoppers envision owning those items. And it motivates them to come.

Here's what separates good listings from great ones — and how FindA.Sale makes it easier.

## 1. Honest, Clear Photos

Bad listing: Dark, blurry photo of furniture.
Great listing: Well-lit photo from two angles showing condition and size.

**Why it matters:** Shoppers decide to come (or not) based on photos. One good shot is worth 100 words.

**FindA.Sale tip:** Upload multiple angles of each item. Our AI learns your style and suggests the most important details to highlight.

## 2. Accurate Descriptions (Not Overselling)

Bad listing: "Mid-century lamp" (could mean anything).
Great listing: "Brass arc floor lamp, 1970s, 7 feet tall, excellent condition, original finial."

**Why it matters:** Specificity builds trust. Shoppers know exactly what they're getting.

**FindA.Sale tip:** Our AI auto-fills descriptions based on category, size, and condition. You review once and publish. No hours of writing.

## 3. Honest Condition Assessment

Bad listing: Hiding damage in photos or descriptions.
Great listing: "Wooden dresser, good bones, minor scratches on top surface, handles intact."

**Why it matters:** Honesty builds reputation. Repeat shoppers trust organizers who are straight with them.

**FindA.Sale tip:** When you upload a photo, our AI assesses condition (excellent, good, fair, poor). Flag if you disagree, and we learn.

## 4. Strategic Pricing

Bad listing: "Priced to move" (vague).
Great listing: "$45 — similar pieces sell for $80–120 on secondary market; we're pricing to sell."

**Why it matters:** Shoppers recognize fair prices and come ready to buy.

**FindA.Sale tip:** See what similar items sold for in your area. Price competitively. FindA.Sale shows you local comps.

## 5. Just Enough Detail

Bad listing: One sentence for each item.
Great listing: 2–3 sentences with dimensions, maker, era, and condition.

**Why it matters:** Detail builds confidence. Shoppers feel informed, not overwhelmed.

**FindA.Sale tip:** Start with our AI description, then add one custom line if the item is special. Just enough to feel personal.

## Putting It Together

A great listing takes time. But with FindA.Sale, it doesn't have to take hours.

Upload photos. Let AI suggest descriptions. Review. Publish. Move on to the next 20 items.

Your next estate sale can be listed in 45 minutes instead of four hours.

Visit finda.sale and try it free. See how much time you save.

—FindA.Sale Team
```

### Day 24 (Wednesday): FAQ Post — "How Does FindA.Sale Work for Organizers?"

**Platform:** Blog + Email (send as FAQ guide)
**Format:** Blog post (500–600 words) styled as Q&A

#### FAQ Blog Post:

**Title:** "How Does FindA.Sale Work? Organizer FAQ"

```
## How do I sign up?

Visit finda.sale and click "Create Organizer Account." You'll need your name, email, and phone number. It takes 2 minutes.

## Do I need a credit card to try beta?

No. Zero credit card required during beta. Just sign up and start creating sales.

## How do I create a sale?

Click "New Sale" in your organizer dashboard. Fill in:
- Sale name and date
- Location
- Brief description (optional)

Then start adding items.

## How do I add items?

Two ways:
1. **Upload photos:** Take a photo of an item, upload it, let AI suggest a title, description, and price. Review and publish (15 seconds).
2. **Manual entry:** Type a title, description, price, and upload a photo.

Most organizers use the AI method to save time.

## How does the AI work?

Upload a photo. Our AI (powered by Google Vision and Claude) analyzes the image and suggests:
- Title (e.g., "Vintage Brass Table Lamp, 1970s")
- Category (e.g., "Lighting")
- Condition (e.g., "Excellent")
- Description (e.g., "Brass construction, original finial, works perfectly")

You review each suggestion and approve, edit, or dismiss it. Nothing publishes without your approval.

## What does it cost?

**During beta:** Free. Zero platform fees.

**After beta:** 5% platform fee on items sold. That's $5 per $100 in sales. No hidden fees.

(Comparison: Competitors charge 13–20%, which means you keep $80–87 per $100. With FindA.Sale, you keep $95.)

## How do shoppers pay?

At the end of your sale, you'll have a checkout link. Shoppers can pay via:
- Credit card
- Debit card
- Digital wallets (Apple Pay, Google Pay)

Payment goes directly to your Stripe account. You keep 95% (after the 5% fee).

## When do I get paid?

Payments from your sale settle to your Stripe account 2 business days after your sale ends. You can withdraw instantly after that.

## What if an item doesn't sell?

No problem. Items that don't sell cost nothing. You don't pay for unsold items. Only items that actually sell.

## Can shoppers reserve items before the sale?

Yes. Shoppers can reserve items online. Reserved items show as "On Hold" so other shoppers know they're not available. You'll see a list of reserved items on your sale dashboard.

## Do I have to sell at my physical location?

No. You can run a fully online-only estate sale. Or hybrid: run a physical sale and accept online orders during the event. Your choice.

## What features will be added after beta?

We're building:
- Treasure hunt mode (turn your sale into a game for shoppers)
- Virtual tours (360 photos of your entire sale)
- Email campaigns (automated reminders to shoppers before your sale)
- Inventory management (track what sold, what didn't, patterns across sales)

All feedback goes into our roadmap.

## How do I give feedback?

Reply to any email from support@finda.sale or click "Send Feedback" in your dashboard. We read everything and respond within 24 hours.

## Can I contact support?

Yes. Email support@finda.sale or call [PATRICK PHONE]. We aim to respond within 24 hours.
```
