### T+3 (Thursday): Blog Post — "What Makes a Great Estate Sale Listing?"

**Platform:** Website blog
**Word Count:** 400–500 words
**Audience:** Estate sale organizers (builds credibility + practical value)

**Title:** What Makes a Great Estate Sale Listing (And How AI Can Help)

**Blog Post Copy:**

```
A great estate sale listing doesn't just describe items. It tells a story. It helps shoppers envision owning those items. And it motivates them to show up.

Here's what separates good listings from great ones — and how FindA.Sale makes creating great listings actually fast.

## 1. Honest, Clear Photos

**Bad listing:** Dark, blurry photo of furniture.
**Great listing:** Well-lit photo from two angles showing condition and size.

Why it matters: Shoppers decide to come (or not) based on photos. One good shot is worth 100 words of description.

FindA.Sale tip: Upload multiple angles of each item. Our AI learns what details matter most and suggests which angles are most important.

## 2. Accurate Descriptions (Not Overselling)

**Bad listing:** "Mid-century lamp" (could mean anything).
**Great listing:** "Brass arc floor lamp, 1970s, 7 feet tall, excellent condition, original finial."

Why it matters: Specificity builds trust. Shoppers know exactly what they're getting before they arrive.

FindA.Sale tip: Our AI auto-fills descriptions based on the item's category, size, and condition. You review once and publish. No hours of writing.

## 3. Honest Condition Assessment

**Bad listing:** Hiding damage in photos or descriptions.
**Great listing:** "Wooden dresser, good bones, minor scratches on top surface, handles intact."

Why it matters: Honesty builds reputation. Repeat shoppers trust organizers who are straight with them.

FindA.Sale tip: When you upload a photo, our AI assesses condition (excellent, good, fair, poor). Disagree? Flag it, and we learn from your feedback.

## 4. Strategic Pricing

**Bad listing:** "Priced to move" (vague).
**Great listing:** "$45 — similar pieces sell for $80–120 on secondary market; we're pricing to sell."

Why it matters: Shoppers recognize fair prices. Fair prices mean shoppers come ready to buy.

FindA.Sale tip: See what similar items typically sell for in your area. Price competitively. That extra context builds trust.

## 5. Just Enough Detail (No Word Vomit)

**Bad listing:** One sentence per item.
**Great listing:** 2–3 sentences with dimensions, maker, era, and condition.

Why it matters: Detail builds confidence. Shoppers feel informed, not overwhelmed.

FindA.Sale tip: Start with our AI description, then add one custom line if the item is special or has a story. Just enough to feel personal, not corporate.

## Putting It Together

A great listing takes time. But with FindA.Sale, it doesn't have to take hours.

Upload photos. Let AI suggest descriptions. Review. Publish. Move on to the next 20 items.

Your next estate sale can be listed in 45 minutes instead of four hours.

Visit finda.sale, create your sale, and try it yourself.

—FindA.Sale Team
```
