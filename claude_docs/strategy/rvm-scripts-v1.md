## AUDIENCE PAIN-POINT BRIEF

**Research Sources:**
- [2026 Estate Sale Outlook: Trends, Prices, and What's in Demand](https://bluemoonestatesales.com/blog/2025/12/18/2026-estate-sale-outlook-trends-prices-and-whats-in-demand/)
- [The Ten Commandments of Pricing - Antique Trader](https://www.antiquetrader.com/collecting-101/the-ten-commandments-of-pricing)
- [12 Best Estate Sale Software Platforms for 2025 | DIYAuctions](https://www.diyauctions.com/learn/estate-sale-software)
- [5 Reasons Why Flea Market Vendors Fail - Shipshewana Trading Place](https://shipshewanatradingplace.com/blog/5-reasons-why-flea-market-vendors-fail/)
- [The Joys And Challenges Of Being An Antique Dealer - FasterCapital](https://fastercapital.com/topics/the-joys-and-challenges-of-being-an-antique-dealer.html)

**Pain Points Confirmed:**
1. **Cataloging burden:** Organizing, pricing, and tagging inventory across multiple sales simultaneously is labor-intensive. Professional operators spend 30-50 hours per estate on manual cataloging and staging.
2. **Pricing paralysis:** Operators struggle to set accurate prices. Emotional pricing (based on what the owner paid, not current market value) leaves inventory unsold. Antique dealers without market research lose 20-40% on misprice.
3. **Buyer reach:** Yard sales and estate sales miss peak buyer traffic. Without marketing reach, Saturday mornings see low foot traffic and slow sales velocity despite quality inventory.
4. **Unsold inventory resolution:** 10-30% of quality items don't sell. Operators spend additional time (and money) liquidating or donating remainder. Post-sale labor adds 15-25 hours.
5. **Solo/small-team burnout:** 1-2 people handle cleaning, pricing, display setup, marketing, checkout, accounting, and teardown — non-paying overhead that eats margins.
6. **Distrust of SaaS:** Operators skeptical of subscription models that promise efficiency but don't directly produce buyers or margin improvement.

## RVM COMPLIANCE NOTE — TCPA/DNC (2026)

**Flag for Legal Review (findasale-legal must sign off before campaign launch):**
- Drop Cowboy RVM delivery method deposits directly to voicemail (recipient never hears a ring). This **may** trigger stricter TCPA compliance requirements than live-call autodialers.
- Confirm: (a) list sourced from business directory (not prior customer opt-in), (b) opt-out mechanism functional (SMS reply to withdraw consent), (c) no calls before 8am or after 9pm recipient's local time, (d) identify caller name/business in voicemail.
- RVM campaigns to business lines generally have lower DNC enforcement risk than consumer lines, but the volume and frequency matter.
- **Recommendation:** Start with 100-200 high-intent prospects (local Grand Rapids estate sale companies with published phone numbers) to validate compliance posture before scaling to 1000+.

### **VERSION A: Curiosity-Gap Hook**

**Persona:** Sarah Chen, former estate sale assistant turned software implementer  
**Backstory (for delivery): Sarah ran her aunt's estate sale for three months. She learned pricing the hard way — wrong. Now she helps operators avoid that pain.**

**Word Count:** 98 words  
**Estimated Runtime:** 35–40 seconds

---

**[SCRIPT — read naturally, as if you're a peer who just thought of something:]**

*[warm, conversational opener — slight smile in voice]*

"Hey, quick question for you: What percentage of your inventory actually sells on day one?" 

*[pause — 2 beats, let the question sink]*

"Because I was just on the phone with another estate sale company here in Grand Rapids, and she said it's closer to 60, maybe 65 percent. The rest? Sits around for months or gets donated. 

I found something that changed that for her. It's a tool we built specifically for people like you — tags inventory from photos, suggests pricing from actual market data, and connects you with local buyers before you even open doors. 

Think it could help? Just text me back the word VIDEO and I'll send you a two-minute walkthrough."

*[soften close — sounds like you're doing them a favor]*

---

**Framework:** Curiosity gap + implied norm violation (if 60% is typical, yours might be lower; if it's lower, you're doing great; if higher, this fixes it) + peer validation + minimal ask

**Why This Works:** Opens with a question that registers as surprising or worthy of self-assessment. The peer example ("another estate sale company here in Grand Rapids") creates social proof without naming names. Positions the tool as a solution to a real, quantified problem.

**Knock-On Effect to Watch:** Caller assumes the prospect is local to GR area. If outreach expands to other regions, customize the geographic callout.

## A/B TEST PLAN

**Phase 1: Validation Cohort (100–150 prospects)**

**Segment 1 (50 local GR estate sale companies):**
- Test Version A (Curiosity Gap) vs. Version E (Local Pride)
- Target: established GR estate sale companies with 3+ years operating history
- Success metric: SMS reply rate (target: 8-12%), link-click rate within 24 hours (target: 40-50% of SMS replies)
- Timeline: Week 1-2 of campaign
- Why these versions: E has hometown advantage; A has proven curiosity-gap psychology. One should outperform for local audience.

**Segment 2 (50 regional antique dealers + small auction houses, broader Midwest):**
- Test Version B (Peer Name-Drop) vs. Version D (Insider Secret)
- Target: dealers/auction operators outside GR metro, 1-5 sales/events per month
- Success metric: SMS reply rate (target: 6-10%), video view start (target: 35-45% of link-clicks)
- Timeline: Week 1-2 of campaign
- Why these versions: B uses peer credential (works outside home region); D speaks to unsold-inventory problem (industry pain that scales nationally).

**Phase 2: Winner Rotation (200–300 prospects)**
- After Week 2: analyze reply rates and link-click rates from Phase 1
- Roll out the highest-performing version (one from Segment 1, one from Segment 2) to new cohorts of 150 each
- Add Version C (Pain-Agitate) to rotation if Phase 1 performance is marginal across both segments
- Success metric: maintain ≥7% SMS reply rate, ≥40% link-click rate, target ≥2 sales conversations per 100 RVMs

**Phase 3: Segment Optimization (400+ prospects)**
- After Phase 2 analysis, build segment-specific rotations:
  - GR locals: Version E + Version A
  - Antique dealers: Version D + Version B
  - Auction house operators: Version B + Version C
  - Flea market coordinators: Version C + Version A (pain + curiosity for transactional operators)
- Track conversion separately by segment
- Success metric: ≥2.5% of RVM recipients move to sales conversation

**Holdback:** Keep 10% of weekly volume as a control group (no RVM) to measure incremental lift if needed.

## CAMPAIGN LOGISTICS & COMPLIANCE

**Delivery Method:** Drop Cowboy RVM (ringless voicemail — deposits straight to VM)

**Caller ID:** Display name: "FindA.Sale" or "Patrick [Last Name]" — must clearly identify caller in voicemail

**Opt-Out Mechanism:** 
- Text "STOP" to [dedicated SMS number] to opt out
- Confirm receipt within 48 hours
- Add to internal DNC list immediately

**Call Windows:**
- Monday–Friday, 9am–6pm recipient's local time (respect timezone boundaries)
- Avoid holidays and weekends
- Avoid back-to-back calls to same number within 30 days (follow TCPA guidance)

**List Source:** 
- Business directory scrape (public estate sale company listings, antique dealer registries, flea market coordinator publicly listed numbers)
- Verify each prospect is a business line before RVM deployment
- Remove any prior customer lines (existing FindA.Sale users) from outreach

**Frequency Cap:** Max 1 RVM per prospect per 60 days in Phase 1-2; if Phase 3 scales, move to quarterly re-touch max
