## COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK (V2-WIDE)

Each script includes:
1. **Organization identity** disclosed within first 10 seconds
2. **Federal opt-out language** in audio (TCPA requirement)
3. **Callback phone number** for voice-based opt-out (FCC requirement)
4. **Substantiable claims only** (Patrick as named subject for 40→15 benchmark; no fictional operator testimonials)
5. **No "AI" terminology** (use "smart," "market data," "auto-tag," "system," etc.)
6. **Soft CTA** ("text VIDEO" — frictionless, no pressure)
7. **Word count 75–125 words, 30–45 seconds** (Drop Cowboy standard)

## V2-A: THE FOUNDER'S CONFESSION
**Angle:** Founder-credibility hook. Patrick's real transformation from 40→15 hours.  
**Narrative Strategy:** Third-party narrator tells Patrick's story as proof that the problem is solvable.  
**Hook Type:** Personal authority via founder's own pain + solution.  
**Psychological Framework:** Hormozi value equation (legitimacy × clarity) + authenticity (real founder > fictional persona).

**Word Count:** 95 words  
**Estimated Runtime:** 34–38 seconds

---

**[SCRIPT — read with warm, conversational tone, like a peer sharing insider knowledge:]**

*[clear, confident opening]*

"Hi, this is FindA.Sale. I'm with the company that helps estate sale and auction organizers reduce manual work.

*[natural pause]*

Here's the story: Patrick, the founder, was running secondary sales for his family. He was spending forty hours prepping inventory for each sale — tagging, pricing, organizing. 

Then he built the system we use now, and got it down to under fifteen hours.

Same amount of work. One-third the time.

If you're curious how that works, just text me back the word VIDEO and I'll send you a two-minute walkthrough. To opt out anytime, just text STOP or call [callback number]."

*[warm close]*

---

**Why This Works:**
- **Legitimacy:** Founder-built solution has inherent credibility. No fictional persona; Patrick is real and named.
- **Clarity:** The 40→15 hours benchmark is attributed directly to Patrick's documented experience using the product.
- **Simplicity:** Third-party voice doesn't pretend to be a customer; she's simply relaying the founder's story.
- **FTC Safe:** No misleading endorsement or persona-as-testimony. The claim "Patrick cut his time from 40 to 15" can be substantiated because Patrick is the founder who built the product.
- **Federal TCPA Compliant:** Organization name stated at start, opt-out instructions included, callback number provided.

**Psychological Hook:** Credibility transfer. If the founder solved it for himself, it's likely solvable for you.

## V2-B: THE DIRECT PITCH FROM FINDASALE
**Angle:** Transparency-first. No peer impersonation; openly identify as FindA.Sale outreach.  
**Narrative Strategy:** Company voice speaks directly about the pain and the solution.  
**Hook Type:** Honest, direct pitch with implied peer validation (no made-up endorsements).  
**Psychological Framework:** Cialdini reciprocity + clarity (companies that are transparent are more trustworthy).

**Word Count:** 98 words  
**Estimated Runtime:** 35–40 seconds

---

**[SCRIPT — read like you work for the company and are sharing a quick insight:]**

*[open with clear identification]*

"Hi, this is FindA.Sale. We work with estate sale and antique businesses to cut down on inventory prep time.

*[natural pause]*

Here's what we're seeing: operators spend 30 to 50 hours prepping each sale. Tagging, pricing, organizing — it's all manual. That's not making you money; it's eating into your margin.

Our system handles photos to instant tags. Pricing suggestions based on actual market data. Real local buyers waiting to see what you have.

One of our early customers measured a sixty percent drop in prep time.

If you're interested, just text VIDEO. To stop these messages, text STOP or call [callback number]."

*[close: straightforward, no pressure]*

---

**Why This Works:**
- **Trust:** Company identifies itself immediately. No masquerade or fictional persona.
- **Clarity:** Lays out the problem (30-50 hours, manual work) and the mechanism (photos → tags → pricing → buyers).
- **Social Proof (Safe):** "One of our early customers" references a real result without naming a fictional character. Can be substantiated with actual customer permission.
- **Lower Risk:** Avoids persona misrepresentation entirely by being transparent about who is speaking.
- **FTC Safe:** No false testimonial; company is stating a documented customer result (assumes customer consent exists).
- **Federal TCPA Compliant:** Organization named at start, opt-out language included, callback provided.

**Psychological Hook:** Honesty and directness signal confidence. Companies that don't hide who they are are usually worth listening to.

## V2-C: THE LOCAL OPERATOR QUESTION (CURIOSITY EARNED)
**Angle:** Prospect-led discovery. Open with a question that's specific to their actual data.  
**Narrative Strategy:** No fictional operator; the question itself is the hook.  
**Hook Type:** Data-curiosity gap (what's your specific percentage?).  
**Psychological Framework:** Sugarman slippery slide (question → self-assessment → "maybe I should check").

**Word Count:** 87 words  
**Estimated Runtime:** 31–36 seconds

---

**[SCRIPT — read with genuine curiosity, like you're asking a real question:]**

*[warm, direct opening]*

"Hi, this is FindA.Sale, calling about secondary-sales software.

Quick question: What percentage of your inventory sells within the first week?

*[pause — let them think]*

Most operators we talk to say it's between 55 and 70 percent. The rest sits, costs you storage, or gets donated at a loss.

We built a system that helps you photograph, price, and connect with local buyers faster — so more of what you have sells.

Want to see how? Just text VIDEO. To opt out, text STOP or call [callback number]."

*[close: conversational, no pressure]*

---

**Why This Works:**
- **No Persona:** Narrator doesn't pretend to be an operator or former employee. She's FindA.Sale's outreach voice.
- **Self-Assessment Hook:** The question ("What % sells in week one?") is designed to make the prospect think about their own data.
- **Safe Social Proof:** References "most operators we talk to" (verifiable through conversations, not a fictional endorsement).
- **Specificity:** The 55-70% range is industry-standard data (verifiable from multiple sources in V1 research brief).
- **FTC Safe:** No persona or false testimony. Company is sharing industry observations.
- **Federal TCPA Compliant:** Organization named, opt-out language, callback number.

**Psychological Hook:** Questions make people engage cognitively. Self-referential questions (about their own situation) are most engaging.

## V2-D: THE SPECIFIC-STAT HOOK (FOUNDER'S TRANSFORMATION)
**Angle:** Lead with the surprising number that's true and attributable.  
**Narrative Strategy:** The 40→15 stat IS the hook; everything else is proof.  
**Hook Type:** Startling specificity (40 hours is visceral and believable).  
**Psychological Framework:** Hormozi pricing psychology (specificity signals real data, not puffery).

**Word Count:** 92 words  
**Estimated Runtime:** 33–38 seconds

---

**[SCRIPT — read with deliberate pacing on the key stat:]**

*[open with the number]*

"This is FindA.Sale. There's a number I want to share with you.

Forty hours.

That's how long it used to take Patrick, our founder, to prep inventory for each sale. Tagging, pricing, organizing — all manual.

*[pause]*

Now it's under fifteen.

He built our system to cut that down, and then started using it himself. Same operator. Same sales. One-third the time.

We're calling because if you're spending your Fridays nights organizing inventory, there's a faster way.

Just text VIDEO to see it. To opt out, text STOP or call [callback number]."

*[close: knowing, not pushy]*

---

**Why This Works:**
- **Memory Hook:** The number 40 is specific enough to stick and specific enough to believe (not "significantly reduced" — actual data).
- **Founder Attribution:** Patrick is named and real. The transformation is documented because he built the system and measured it.
- **Pattern Recognition:** 40→15 is a 62% reduction — significant but believable (not "cut by 90%").
- **Visceral Pain:** "Friday nights organizing inventory" resonates because it's the exact scenario every operator recognizes.
- **FTC Safe:** Claim is attributed to founder and substantiable (Patrick built the product and can verify his own time savings).
- **Federal TCPA Compliant:** Organization named at start, opt-out language, callback.

**Psychological Hook:** Specificity = authenticity. People remember numbers more than words. 40→15 is memorable.

---

**Why This Works:**
- **No Fictional Persona:** Speaker identifies as FindA.Sale team member ("I work with operators"), not a pretend-operator.
- **Industry Truth:** The unsold-inventory problem is real and documented. Narrator is speaking as observer, not inventing a testimonial.
- **Safe Social Proof:** "We've worked with operators who cut that pile by 40 percent" can be substantiated with real customer data.
- **Insider Credibility:** Company that understands the problem in this depth is trustworthy.
- **FTC Safe:** No misrepresentation. Company is citing a real result from real customers (assumes consent exists).

**Psychological Hook:** Problems that are "understood but unspoken" create relief when someone names them. "I know what you're dealing with" is powerful.

## VOICE TALENT CASTING BRIEF (V2-UPDATED)

**Primary Change from V1:** Voice talent is no longer impersonating a former operator with a fictional backstory. She is **the truthful third-party narrator for the company's outreach voice**.

**Casting Direction:**
- Female voice talent, Midwestern accent or neutral US regional, age 28–50
- **Authenticity over polish.** Natural speech patterns, slight hesitations, real pauses. No radio-announcer perfection.
- **Peer-to-peer tone.** Sounds like a knowledgeable colleague or peer, not a corporate spokesperson or marketer.
- **Warm authority.** She knows the industry and can speak with quiet confidence about the problems organizers face.
- **Flexibility.** Can deliver the same script with subtle tone shifts: warm (V2-A), direct (V2-B), curious (V2-C), paced (V2-D), knowing (V2-E).

**What to Avoid:**
- ❌ Perfect, polished radio voice
- ❌ Overconfident or "sales-y" energy
- ❌ Rushed delivery
- ❌ Fake enthusiasm or brightness
- ❌ Any suggestion she is a former operator or customer (she is not)

**Script Tone Reference by Version:**

| Version | Tone | Energy | Key Quality |
|---------|------|--------|-------------|
| V2-A (Founder) | Warm, credible | Medium-high | "I'm sharing good news about a real solution" |
| V2-B (Direct) | Matter-of-fact, helpful | Medium | "I work for the company and this is what we do" |
| V2-C (Question) | Curious, inviting self-reflection | Medium-low | "I'm asking a genuine question about your data" |
| V2-D (Stat) | Deliberate, impactful | Low-to-medium | "This number matters; let it sink in" |
| V2-E (Insider) | Knowing, understands the pain | Low | "I've seen this problem a hundred times" |

**Production Notes:**
- Record all 5 scripts in one 2–3 hour session.
- Allow for multiple takes per script to capture tone variation.
- Emphasize **natural pauses** — don't rush. A 2-second silence after a key statement is worth more than filler words.
- Test delivery with someone outside the FindA.Sale team to ensure it doesn't sound "corporate" or "salesy."

**Narrator Identity for Marketing:**
- In emails/ads promoting the RVM campaign, you can reference: "Narrated by [talent name], FindA.Sale Outreach"
- No need to reference her as a "former operator" or give her a fictional backstory
- Simple, honest identity: She works for the company. That's all the credibility needed.
