# FindA.Sale Customer Champion Pricing Review
**Date:** 2026-03-19
**Role:** Customer Experience & Sentiment Analyst
**Perspective:** How organizers and shoppers will FEEL about two proposed pricing decisions

---

## DECISION 1: Organizer Fee Structure — 10% free, 8% any paid tier

**The Proposal:**
- **SIMPLE (Free tier):** 10% platform fee
- **PRO ($29/mo):** 8% platform fee (instead of 10%)
- **TEAMS ($79/mo):** 8% platform fee (instead of 10%)

*(Note: Advisory Board recommends 10%/7%/5% tiered structure, but we're evaluating the 10%/8%/8% variant)*

---

### 1. Customer Sentiment Forecast

**Rating: NEUTRAL, trending POSITIVE with conditions**

**Why:**
The 2% fee reduction ($20 savings per $1,000 sold) is mathematically meaningful at typical estate sale volumes ($5k–$15k per sale), but psychologically, it feels like table stakes rather than a compelling upgrade driver. Organizers have been conditioned by EstateSales.NET ($99/sale), MaxSold (15-30% commission), and eBay (12.9% all-in) to expect high transaction costs. An 8% fee doesn't feel special—it feels fair.

**The mental math organizers will do:**
- "I sell $10,000 per sale. At 10%, I lose $1,000. At 8%, I lose $800. That's $200 per sale, $2,400/year if I do 12 sales."
- "But $29/month is $348/year. So I'm paying $348 to save $2,400. The math works... but barely."
- "What if I only do 3 sales? That's $600 saved, but still $348 out of pocket for the subscription. Feels like a break-even."

**Sentiment drivers:**
- **Positive:** The fee reduction is real and quantifiable. Organizers love transparency—they'll respect a tiered model if the communication is crystal clear.
- **Negative:** Feels incremental, not transformational. The magic threshold for upgrade drivers is usually 3–5% fee reduction, not 2%.
- **Uncertain:** Organizers won't know what version they're on. Confusion in onboarding = churn.

---

### 2. The Angry Organizer Facebook Post

**"Why should I pay for a lower fee?"**

> "Just ran my first sale on FindA.Sale and they're taking 10%—that's $1,000 on a $10k sale. Then they tell me if I pay $29/month, it drops to 8%. So I have to PAY THEM to keep more of MY OWN MONEY? That's backwards. EstateSales.NET charges $99 per sale, flat. At least I know what I'm paying up front. I'm not subscribing to software I might not use every month. This feels like they're nickel-and-diming me twice—once on the fee, once on the subscription."

---

### 3. Recommended Communication Strategy

**Messaging Architecture:**

**HEADLINE (Pricing Page / Onboarding):**
> "Keep More of Your Sale Revenue. Upgrade to PRO and drop your platform fee from 10% to 8%."

**SUBHEADING:**
> "At a $10,000 sale, you save $200 per event. Most organizers break even on the subscription in their second sale."

**CALCULATOR (Interactive, On Pricing Page):**
```
What's your typical sale size?
[Slider: $1,000 — $100,000]

PRO Savings at $10,000:
  • SIMPLE: You pay $1,000 (10%)
  • PRO: You pay $800 (8%)
  • Annual savings (if 12 sales): $2,400
  • PRO cost: $348/year
  • Net annual benefit: $2,052
```

**EMAIL TO EXISTING ORGANIZERS (2 weeks before fee change):**
> Subject: Your Platform Fee is About to Change — Here's How Much You'll Save

> Hi [Organizer Name],

> We're rolling out tiered platform fees based on subscription level. Here's what's changing for you:

> **If you stay on SIMPLE (free):** Your platform fee remains 10%.
> **If you upgrade to PRO ($29/month):** Your platform fee drops to 8%.

> **Your Estimated Savings (if you do 6 sales/year at $10k each):**
> • Without upgrade: $6,000 in fees
> • With PRO: $4,800 in fees + $348 subscription = $5,148 total
> • Net savings: $852/year (after subscription cost)

> This is a real financial benefit, not a gimmick. The subscription also unlocks batch operations, analytics, and unlimited sales.

> Questions? Reply to this email or check out our FAQ.

**What makes this work:**
- Lead with savings, not the subscription price
- Use a concrete calculator so organizers do their own math (more trustworthy than our claims)
- Acknowledge the subscription cost upfront (transparency builds trust)
- Include the break-even threshold (organizers will self-segment)

---

### 4. One Thing That Makes It Feel BETTER (Without Changing Price)

**Lock in the 8% fee for early adopters (forever).**

> "Upgrade to PRO by [Date], and we'll lock your platform fee at 8% for life. If FindA.Sale raises fees in the future, yours stays at 8%."

**Why this works:**
- Scarcity + urgency (early adopters get a permanent advantage)
- Removes the risk of "we'll raise the fee later" (organizers distrust SaaS pricing creep)
- Converts this from "paying for a discount" to "getting in on a founder's rate"
- No revenue loss—most organizers will never see a future fee increase anyway

**Alternative:** Offer a 1-year lock-in at 8% for PRO subscribers (not forever, but sufficient to feel special)

## DECISION 2: 5% Buyer Premium on Auction Items Only

**The Proposal:**
Shoppers who win auction bids pay an additional 5% on top of the hammer price (the final bid amount). This applies ONLY to auction purchases, not standard fixed-price estate sale purchases.

---

### 1. Customer Sentiment Forecast

**Rating: CAUTIOUSLY POSITIVE, with significant UX risk**

**Why:**
A 5% buyer premium is reasonable by auction house standards (live auctioneers charge 15-25%), but it will surprise shoppers at checkout—and surprise always breeds resentment, even if the price is fair in isolation.

**The mental math shoppers will do:**
- "This vase went for $150 in the auction. Plus 5%... that's $157.50. Wait, why am I paying MORE than what I bid? I thought that was the price!"
- "On eBay, there's no surprise premium. The bid IS the price. Why is FindA.Sale different?"
- "5% on a $2,000 win = $100. That's meaningful. I wasn't planning on that."

**Sentiment drivers:**
- **Positive:** Shoppers understand auction houses charge premiums. If communicated early and often, it feels normal.
- **Negative:** If the 5% appears for the first time at checkout, shoppers feel deceived and may abandon the purchase.
- **Critical:** Auction vs. non-auction split creates confusion. Shoppers will ask "Why do auctions have a fee but regular sales don't?" The answer (cost recovery for auction infrastructure) isn't obvious.

---

### 2. The Angry Shopper Reddit / Facebook Post

**"Surprise Fees on Auction Wins"**

> "Just lost a bidding war on a vintage chair at $180 and then got hit with a 5% buyer premium at checkout—$189 total. I get that auction houses charge premiums, but FindA.Sale didn't make THAT clear when I was placing bids. The bid total should be the final price. If there's an extra fee, it should be shown BEFORE I place the bid, not when I'm hitting 'confirm purchase.' Also, why do regular estate sales have no fee but auctions do? Seems like FindA.Sale is trying to nickel-and-dime treasure hunters. Switching to EstateSales.NET where I know what I'm paying upfront."

**Key pain points in this post:**
1. Surprise at checkout (too late to back out)
2. Unclear WHY the split exists (auction vs. standard sale)
3. Comparison to competitors (EstateSales.NET doesn't charge shoppers)
4. Feeling "nickel-and-dimed" (cumulative perception of multiple fees)

---

### 3. Recommended Communication Strategy

**CRITICAL: Communicate the 5% BEFORE the shopper places a bid, not after.**

**Bidding Interface (On the Auction Listing):**
```
Vintage Oak Chair — Auction Ends in 2 hours

Current bid: $150
Your bid: [________]

⚠️  Auction Purchase Fee: 5%
    If you win at $180, you'll pay $189 total
    (includes 5% buyer premium)

[Place Bid]
```

**Bid Confirmation Modal (After placing bid):**
> "Your bid of $180 is placed!"
> "If you win, your final total: $189 (includes 5% buyer premium)"
> "This is different from standard estate sales (no premium)."

**Auction Listing Description (Top of page):**
> "This is an auction. Winning bids include a 5% buyer premium, standard in the auction industry. See FAQ for examples."

**FAQ Section (Create if not exists):**
> **Q: Why does this auction have a 5% fee but regular sales don't?**
> A: Auction infrastructure (real-time bidding, soft-close, bid notifications, fraud prevention) costs more to operate than fixed-price sales. We pass that cost to auction winners as a 5% premium, not to all shoppers. Fixed-price sales remain fee-free for shoppers.

> **Q: Is 5% normal?**
> A: Yes. Live auction houses charge 15-25% buyer's premium. Online auctions (Mercari, Whatnot, Catawiki) charge 5-12%. 5% is on the lower end.

> **Q: Can I see the final price before I bid?**
> A: Yes. The bid total shown includes the 5% premium automatically. What you see is what you'll pay (if you win).

**Email to Shoppers (On Auction Feature Launch):**
> Subject: New Feature: Live Auctions Now Available on FindA.Sale

> We've launched auctions—shoppers can bid against each other on high-demand items, and organizers can maximize revenue on rare finds.

> **One thing to know:** Auction purchases include a 5% buyer premium, standard in the auction industry. Fixed-price estate sale purchases remain fee-free for shoppers. You'll see the final price (with premium included) before you place your bid.

---

### 4. One Thing That Makes It Feel BETTER (Without Changing Price)

**Transparency + Gamification combo:**

**Option A: "Fee Offset" Streak Bonus**
When a shopper wins an auction and pays the 5% premium, award them streak points worth 5% of the premium amount back. (E.g., $180 win + $9 premium → earn 9 streak points → redeemable for $0.50 toward next purchase or toward Hunt Pass.)

**Why this works:**
- Softens the sting of the premium (feels like a partial refund)
- Drives Hunt Pass adoption (gamification path)
- Makes the fee feel conditional, not permanent

**Option B: "Auction Success" Badge + Leaderboard**
Shoppers who win 3+ auctions get a "Master Bidder" badge. Auction winners appear on a leaderboard. No monetary reward, but social proof and status.

**Why this works:**
- Reframes the auction fee as an entry into an exclusive club
- Shoppers feel special, not cheated
- Organic marketing (shoppers brag about badges)

**Option C: "First Auction Free" (Limited-time offer)**
"Your first auction purchase has NO buyer premium. Win your first auction for free, then standard 5% applies afterward."

**Why this works:**
- Removes trial friction (shoppers experience auction at zero cost)
- Converts first-time auction bidders into repeat users
- Fair to new shoppers, incentivizes adoption

## COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS: How These Two Decisions INTERACT

**Risk:** If organizers feel nickeled-on-fees (Decision 1: 10% base, 8% if subscribed) AND shoppers feel nickeled-on-auctions (Decision 2: 5% buyer premium), the cumulative perception is **"FindA.Sale is expensive."**

**Mitigation:**
1. **Lead with savings for organizers** (Decision 1), not the fee structure. Emphasize what they GAIN (batch ops, analytics, savings), not what they PAY.
2. **Lead with transparency for shoppers** (Decision 2). Show the 5% upfront, everywhere. Surprise is the killer.
3. **Create a narrative bridge:** "We keep FindA.Sale free for shoppers on fixed-price sales. Auction premiums are industry-standard and low compared to live auctioneers. Organizer fees are competitive and tiered by usage."

---

## FINAL RECOMMENDATIONS

### Decision 1 Approval: ✅ YES, with communication plan
The 10%/8%/8% fee structure is defensible and fair. The 2% savings is mathematically sound. Communication is critical—without the calculator and break-even messaging, organizers will perceive this as "paying to lower their own fees," which is toxic.

**Go-live condition:** Pricing page includes an interactive calculator showing organizer ROI.

### Decision 2 Approval: ✅ YES, with UX safeguards
The 5% buyer premium is reasonable, but it MUST appear in four places before checkout: (1) auction listing, (2) bid placement, (3) bid confirmation, (4) checkout review. Surprise at checkout is unacceptable.

**Go-live condition:** 5% premium is shown before the bid is placed, not after.

### Strategic Risk: Cumulative Perception
Both decisions involve fees on top of fees. If communicated poorly, FindA.Sale feels expensive to both organizers and shoppers. Recommend a single "pricing narrative" document that explains all fees in one place, with competitive benchmarking and ROI models. Shoppers shouldn't see it (they see zero fees on fixed-price sales); organizers should see it in onboarding, email, and FAQ.
