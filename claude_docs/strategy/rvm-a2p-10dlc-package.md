## PART 1: RESEARCH SUMMARY & 2026 CONTEXT

### Current Regulatory Landscape (April 2026)

**CRITICAL — Rabbit v. Rohrman Ruling (March 30, 2026)**
The U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Illinois has ruled that SMS messages are **calls subject to the TCPA's Do Not Call (DNC) subsection**. This dramatically increases compliance risk:
- Every unscrubbed marketing text sent to a DNC-registered number = potential **$500/message statutory claim** (rising to $1,500/message for willful violations)
- National DNC Registry scrubbing is now **mandatory before any SMS send**
- The ruling is specific to the Northern District of Illinois (Seventh Circuit appellate timeline = 18-30 months), but creates significant litigation precedent

**Recommendation for FindA.Sale:** Your SMS auto-reply flow is triggered by **recipient consent** (they initiated by texting "VIDEO" to a dedicated number after hearing a voicemail), which provides strong TCPA protection. However, any future broadcast campaigns targeting organizers MUST scrub against the National DNC Registry before send.

**A2P 10DLC Compliance Status (2026)**
- **Mandatory Registration:** Since February 1, 2025, carriers (T-Mobile, AT&T, Verizon, US Cellular) block **100% of unregistered A2P traffic**. No registration = no delivery.
- **Campaign Registry (TCR):** Single centralized database for all US A2P SMS registration. All carriers enforce TCR records.
- **Brand Registration:** $4/month (or $48/year) for standard brand; $2/month for low-volume (<6,000 texts/month)
- **Campaign Registration:** $10/month per campaign
- **Timeline:** Brand registration 1–3 business days; Campaign registration 3–7 business days (up to 10–15 during peak periods)
- **Per-Message Carrier Surcharge:** $0.003–$0.005/SMS (AT&T, T-Mobile, Verizon)

## PART 4: RISK MITIGATION & COMPLIANCE NOTES

### 1. **TCPA Compliance (Post-Rabbit v. Rohrman, March 2026)**

**Current Risk Landscape:**
- Auto-reply triggered by recipient consent (they text "VIDEO" first) = **strong TCPA protection** ✓
- However, any **future broadcast campaigns** to cold organizers require:
  - Written prior express consent (email, form, phone verification) or
  - Established business relationship + reasonable expectation of SMS
  - National DNC Registry scrubbing before every send (newly critical post-Rabbit v. Rohrman)

**Recommendation:** Document your opt-in process. If prospects are cold-called before receiving an RVM, ensure the voicemail script explicitly states "By texting VIDEO, you agree to receive FindA.Sale product information. Message and data rates apply. Standard opt-out rights apply."

---

### 2. **SMS Carrier Throughput Limits**

T-Mobile enforces **per-day throughput limits** based on brand trust score:
- **Low-Volume Brand:** 100/day
- **Standard Brand (recommended):** 1,000/day
- **Advanced Brand:** 5,000–10,000/day

For the auto-reply use case (replies triggered by prospect action), throughput is rarely a constraint. However, if future broadcast campaigns send >1,000 marketing texts/day, you'll need to upgrade to Advanced brand tier.

---

### 3. **Domain & Email Reputation**

TCR automated verification checks:
- **finda.sale domain:** Must have active SSL, valid WHOIS registration, and age >30 days for best results. ✓ (currently valid)
- **deseee@yahoo.com email:** Should have clean email reputation (no spam flags, bounce history). If brand registration fails due to email reputation, switch to a branded email (contact@finda.sale) after verifying it has no spam flags.

---

### 4. **Opt-Out Compliance**

- All messages must include a way to opt out (STOP keyword mandatory)
- Opt-out requests must be honored within 30 days
- Keep records of all opt-out requests for TCPA defense

---

### 5. **Privacy Policy & Data Handling**

TCR requires a published Privacy Policy. Ensure https://finda.sale/privacy or /terms clearly states:
- How SMS data is collected, used, and retained
- How long numbers are kept after opt-out
- Contact method for privacy inquiries (email: support@finda.sale or phone)

## APPENDIX: GLOSSARY & REFERENCE LINKS

### Key Acronyms:
- **A2P:** Application-to-Person (automated SMS from systems to humans)
- **10DLC:** 10-Digit Long Code (standard 10-digit phone number for SMS)
- **TCR:** The Campaign Registry (centralized SMS compliance database maintained by carriers)
- **TCPA:** Telephone Consumer Protection Act (US law governing telemarketing and SMS consent)
- **BYOC:** Bring Your Own Carrier (customer manages their own SMS provider account)
- **DNC:** Do Not Call Registry (National Registry of consumers who opted out of marketing calls/SMS)
- **RVM:** Ringless Voicemail (voicemail delivered directly without ringing the phone)
