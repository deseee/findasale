# Weekly Competitor Intel — 2026-03-26

---

## Competitor Updates

**EstateSales.NET** — No new feature announcements this week. March 10 app update (buyer push notifications for favorited sales) remains the most recent change. Pattern unchanged: buyer-experience investment only, organizer tooling static. The gap stays open.

**MaxSold** — January 2026 Yelp reviews confirm ongoing app quality issues: buggy photo uploads, no help UI, manual lot progression required. Smart AI cataloging is their differentiator but the UX surrounding it remains rough. No new feature announcements detected.

**EstateSales.org** — No changes detected.

**Rosy (gorosy.co)** — Payment processing feature confirmed as "coming soon" — not yet live. Currently no in-app checkout. Rosy's strengths: contract generation, shopper email campaigns, dynamic pricing suggestions (eBay/Facebook trend-based). Weakness: no buyer-facing sale pages, no PWA, and no payment processing yet. SoftwareSuggest and SoftwareAdvice now listing Rosy in 2026 reviews — gaining visibility.

**PROSALE** — No new changes. Still the expensive incumbent ($99–$329/mo). SoftwareAdvice 2026 pricing page live, which means buyers are actively comparing it.

**New Entrants** — No new entrants detected this week. EstateSales.org's buyer app (Estate Sale Finder) was updated March 16 but remains shopper-only.

## Market Signals

Blue Moon 2026 outlook post confirms: Boomer downsizing is the primary growth driver; death-related liquidation up to 57% of estate sales (from 49% in 2021). 1990s/early 2000s fashion driving shopper lineups. Large formal furniture is soft. Demand is strong; supply of quality sales (well-run, well-marketed) remains the constraint. Independent organizers who look professional win.

EstateSales.NET Trustpilot/ComplaintsBoard complaints active: unexpected auction fees are the #1 shopper complaint, which also damages organizer trust in the platform.

## Tech Signals

**Next.js PWA (native, no third-party):** Next.js App Router now supports PWA manifest natively — no next-pwa or Serwist required for basic install. Next.js 16 PWA article confirmed: offline support patterns maturing. Relevant to FindA.Sale install-rate goals.

**Stripe Embedded Checkout (Server Actions):** 2026 best practice confirmed: EmbeddedCheckout component via @stripe/react-stripe-js, Server Action architecture, no redirect. FindA.Sale hasn't implemented checkout yet — this is the right approach when we do.

## Top 3 Actionable Insights

1. **Rosy still has no payment processing.** Every organizer using Rosy is still cobbling together Venmo/Square/cash at checkout. FindA.Sale's Stripe Connect integration (when live) is a direct differentiator against the most modern competitor in our lane.

2. **"Unexpected fees" is the #1 complaint about EstateSales.NET's marketplace model.** This is a real organizer trust issue — they list a sale, shoppers complain about fees, and the organizer absorbs the reputational damage. Transparent, organizer-controlled pricing is a concrete positioning angle.

3. **Independent organizers need to look franchise-level without the franchise cost.** Blue Moon's 2026 content is explicitly marketing franchise ownership as a competitive advantage. FindA.Sale's counter-story: you get franchise-grade tools without franchise fees or franchise rules.
