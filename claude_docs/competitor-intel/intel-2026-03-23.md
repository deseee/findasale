# Weekly Competitor Intel — 2026-03-23

---

## Competitor Updates

**EstateSales.NET** — App updated March 10, 2026. Feature this week: push notifications for favorites. Buyers who favorite a sale now receive a push notification the day before it starts. 50% opt-in rate reported. This is entirely buyer-facing — zero new organizer tooling. The pattern continues: EstateSales.NET is a buyer-experience company. The organizer gap remains wide open.

**MaxSold** — No new feature announcements. Yelp complaints active through March 2026 in Atlanta and Los Angeles: pickup logistics, item misrepresentation. No product response visible.

**EstateSales.org** — No changes detected. Status quo.

**Rosy (gorosy.co) — NEW ENTRANT TO WATCH:** Modern estate sale software surfaced this week. Key features: AI-powered dynamic pricing suggestions based on eBay/Facebook trends, personalized email campaigns with shopper list growth tools, automatic post-sale review requests, and transparent client reporting. Subscription model. This is the most modern-feeling competitor in the organizer-software lane — potentially the same ICP as FindA.Sale. Different from PROSALE (which is heavy desktop POS); Rosy is lighter and relationship-focused.

**PROSALE** — Pricing confirmed: $99/mo (management only) → $329/mo (full e-commerce). Still web-based, no PWA. Positive reviews but users note slow photo uploads. This is the expensive incumbent Rosy and FindA.Sale are both disrupting.

## Market Signals

Baby Boomer downsizing continues as the primary estate sale demand driver. Gen Z and Millennial buyers are entering peak purchasing years with strong vintage/mid-century appetite — mid-century modern, 90s fashion, and vintage tools are moving fast. Demand is up; supply of quality sales is constrained. Organizers who run efficient, well-marketed sales have a strong edge right now.

Franchise consolidation (Blue Moon's AI rollout now complete as of last week) continues to widen the gap between franchise operators and independents. No new franchise entrants detected.

---

## Tech Signals

**Next.js 16 + PWA:** Native PWA support improving in Next.js ecosystem; Serwist emerging as cleaner alternative to next-pwa for offline/install functionality. Relevant for FindA.Sale's install-rate goals.

**Stripe Embedded Checkout:** Stripe is pushing Embedded Checkout hard — iframe/web component that keeps users on-domain while Stripe handles PCI. Link Authentication Element auto-fills returning Stripe users, reported +10% conversion. Worth evaluating for FindA.Sale checkout flow.

---

## Top 3 Actionable Insights

1. **Rosy is the most direct modern competitor to FindA.Sale's organizer-software lane.** It's lighter than PROSALE, AI-assisted, and relationship-focused. Understand where Rosy falls short (no PWA, no real-time shopper-facing sale pages, no Stripe Connect) and use that as positioning language.

2. **EstateSales.NET is doubling down on buyers, not organizers.** Their push notification feature is good for shopper engagement — but organizers still get nothing new. Every week they don't fix organizer tools is another week FindA.Sale's pitch gets easier.

3. **Stripe Embedded Checkout + Link auth is a conversion lever worth testing.** If FindA.Sale ever puts paid features behind a checkout, the Link auth pre-fill could meaningfully improve conversion with minimal dev work.
